# Civilizational Mercy Research Program

**Status:** Research agenda v1  
**Purpose:** Convert the civilizational-mercy synthesis into a source-hardened comparative research program and deployable institutional designs.

---

## 1. Master question

> **How have civilizations attempted to restrain human power over beings with little or no ability to retaliate—and which combinations of worldview, story, ritual, law, institution, and accountability have produced the strongest durable protection?**

This program studies animals as the cleanest initial audit while connecting the findings to children, prisoners, civilians, minorities, debtors, workers, ecosystems, and future generations.

---

## 2. Core hypotheses

### H1 — Power-scaling hypothesis

As human coordination, surplus, technology, and administrative reach increase, moral distance and the capacity for organized harm increase unless restraint scales with them.

### H2 — Moral-visibility hypothesis

A being is more vulnerable to abuse when its interiority, suffering, identity, and relationship network are cognitively or institutionally hidden.

### H3 — Unseen-witness hypothesis

Internalized accountability before a morally concerned God can constrain hidden wrongdoing beyond the reach of reputation and law, but effects depend on formation, interpretation, and whether divine authority is captured by human power.

### H4 — Story-formation hypothesis

Narratives learned in childhood can create durable bright-line prohibitions and moral perception before abstract reasoning develops.

### H5 — Redundant-constraint hypothesis

No single mechanism—empathy, fear, law, education, surveillance, or religion—is sufficient. Durable protection requires a redundant stack.

### H6 — ʿAsabiyyah direction hypothesis

High group cohesion increases collective capability but does not determine moral direction. Universalized mercy and law are needed to prevent solidarity from becoming domination of outsiders.

### H7 — Full-life welfare hypothesis

Final-moment slaughter standards are insufficient proxies for animal welfare. Moral auditing must cover the complete life and supply chain.

### H8 — Digital-amphitheater hypothesis

Digital platforms both expose cruelty and create status, audience, and monetization incentives that can increase performative abuse.

## 3. Source architecture

Every claim should be separated into five layers:

1. **Revelation / primary moral text**
2. **Interpretation / jurisprudence / commentary**
3. **Historical implementation**
4. **Modern empirical evidence**
5. **Public advocacy claim**

A source at one layer cannot automatically prove another.

Example:

- A hadith may establish a normative teaching.
- It does not prove universal Muslim practice.
- A historical institution may show implementation.
- It does not prove the institution was unique.
- A welfare study may test a method.
- It does not settle theology by itself.

## 5. Comparative moral-history agenda

Evaluate each tradition through the same matrix:

1. What is an animal ontologically?
2. Does it possess value beyond human utility?
3. What uses are permitted?
4. What suffering is prohibited?
5. What stories form children and adults?
6. What is the accountability mechanism?
7. Which institutions operationalized the ethic?
8. Where did economics or power defeat the teaching?
9. How broad is the protected animal circle?
10. How has the tradition adapted to modern industrial systems?

Traditions and systems:

- Judaism;
- Christianity;
- Islam;
- Jainism;
- Buddhism;
- Hindu traditions;
- Confucian traditions;
- Daoist traditions;
- Indigenous traditions from multiple regions;
- Greco-Roman philosophy;
- modern secular sentience ethics;
- utilitarian animal ethics;
- rights-based animal ethics;
- capabilities approaches;
- veterinary and welfare science;
- anarchist and ecological critiques of civilization.

Avoid winner-selection before evidence collection.

- belief in supernatural monitoring;
- moralizing gods and large-scale cooperation;

Key truth guardrail:

> Association is not deterministic prediction. A child who harms an animal is not automatically a future murderer, but serious cruelty is a protection and assessment signal.

## 8. Slaughter and full-life welfare agenda

### Empirical questions

- time to loss of consciousness by species and method;
- pain associated with neck incision;
- stunning efficacy and failure modes;
- reversible stunning standards;
- restraint stress;
- visual and auditory fear triggers;
- blade sharpness and operator competence;
- cattle-specific false aneurysm concerns;
- post-cut handling;
- death verification;
- transport and lairage;
- chronic farm-life harms.

### Theological/legal questions

- what is fixed versus method-dependent?
- how is iḥsān operationalized under new evidence?
- which stunning methods are accepted by which authorities?
- what makes an animal legally alive at the cut?
- how should doubt and welfare risk be balanced?
- can ṭayyib and maqāṣid reasoning support full-life certification?

### Public rule

Never claim either:

- “halal slaughter is always painless”; or
- “all non-stun slaughter is identical torture.”

State conditions, evidence, species differences, and uncertainty.

## 9. Wang Wang case ledger

Maintain a three-tier evidence ledger:

### Confirmed

Only facts supported by official statements or multiple credible reports.

### Credibly reported but not officially confirmed

Claims with identifiable sourcing that remain provisional.

### Viral / unverified

Claims circulating without adequate evidence, including details that may be emotionally compelling but should not be repeated as fact.

Research dimensions:

- official investigation;
- ages and legal treatment of minors;
- guardian accountability;
- school response;
- animal-protection law in China;
- Chinese public opinion;
- Chinese animal-welfare movements;
- platform distribution;
- whether monetization claims have evidence;
- policy follow-up;
- credible Chinese-language sources.

Anti-propaganda rule:

> One atrocity is evidence about a failure event, not proof of the moral essence of a people.

## 10. Institutional design experiments

### Experiment A — Children’s story curriculum

Compare:

- punishment-only story;
- empathy-only story;
- integrated story combining mercy, duty, unseen accountability, and action steps.

Measure:

- welfare knowledge;
- emotional empathy;
- cognitive empathy;
- intervention intention;
- retention;
- behavior under peer pressure.

### Experiment B — Bystander protocol

Teach children a memorable sequence:

> **Stop if safe. Leave the group. Protect the animal. Get an adult. Report—do not perform or repost.**

Test recall and simulated decision-making.

### Experiment C — Graphic versus dignity-centered advocacy

Compare effects on:

- action completion;
- retraumatization;
- sharing behavior;
- desensitization;
- policy support;
- trust;
- long-term retention.

### Experiment D — Faith-adaptable curriculum

Create Islamic, Christian, Jewish, Buddhist, Hindu/Jain, Chinese-tradition, and secular humane-education modules sharing a universal protection standard.

### Experiment E — Platform evidence pipeline

Prototype removal + secure evidence preservation + investigator referral without public recirculation.

## 13. Research integrity rules

- Prefer primary scripture and official legal texts for normative claims.
- Use qualified scholarship for interpretation.
- Use veterinary and welfare science for empirical claims.
- Cite contrary evidence.
- Record uncertainty.
- Distinguish historical baseline from ideal perfection.
- Avoid country-level essentialism.
- Do not use animals as a rhetorical weapon while ignoring inconvenient suffering.
- Update conclusions when evidence changes.
- Preserve claims in source ledgers before deploying them as public certainties.

---

## 14. Ultimate objective

> **Design a civilizational operating system in which every increase in capability is met by an equal or greater increase in moral visibility, responsibility, restraint, mercy, and correction.**

The goal is not to make humanity powerless.

It is to make power trustworthy.
