# People of the Qiblah Civilizational Commons — Research and Implementation Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-07-14  
**Status:** Active research and build agenda  
**Canonical parent:** `canon/PEOPLE_OF_THE_QIBLAH_CIVILIZATIONAL_COMMONS.md`

## Mission

Design and validate a non-state-owned, multilingual, provenance-aware civilizational commons that protects legitimate Islamic difference while preventing takfir, dehumanization, propaganda, historical caricature, and state or sectarian capture.

The project is not a third sect or doctrinal merger. It is a truth and cooperation architecture.

## Strategic Hypothesis

The bridge becomes durable only when it combines five layers that previous efforts usually separated:

1. **A protected covenant** defining the civilizational floor.
2. **A truth architecture** for disputed history and doctrine.
3. **A shared-work layer** producing visible common benefit.
4. **A media and AI immune system** resisting algorithmic sectarianism.
5. **An anti-capture institution** resilient to states, donors, founders, and geopolitical cycles.

## Highest-Leverage Question

> **Why did previous intra-Islamic bridge efforts remain declarations, scholarly networks, conferences, or state-linked institutions rather than becoming a distributed civilizational commons—and what architecture would make the next attempt resilient?**

## Workstream 1 — Ahl al-Qiblah Source Universe

### Objective

Establish the legal, theological, and historical meanings of *Ahl al-Qiblah* across major Islamic traditions.

### Research requirements

- earliest known uses of the category;
- treatment in Sunni theological and legal schools;
- treatment in Twelver, Zaydi, Ismaili, and Ibadi sources;
- relationship to takfir, grave sin, bid'ah, political rebellion, and communal rights;
- variations in who is included or excluded;
- modern reuse by scholars, states, and institutions;
- and whether the phrase can support a protected commons without concealing real boundary disputes.

### Output

`source-ledgers/ahl-al-qiblah-legal-historical-source-ledger.md`

### Falsifier

If the term's historical usage is too inconsistent or exclusionary to support the proposed civilizational floor, the institution must retain the mission while reconsidering the public name.

## Workstream 3 — Disputed History Intelligence Protocol

### Objective

Create a repeatable method that can handle load-bearing disputes without propaganda, false consensus, or humiliation.

### First case cluster

# Saqifa → Ghadir Khumm → First Fitna → Karbala

### Required layers

1. event chronology;
2. earliest source universe;
3. strongest Sunni interpretation;
4. strongest Shia interpretation;
5. Ibadi, Zaydi, Ismaili, and academic perspectives where material;
6. source dates and chains;
7. political and institutional context;
8. later memory formation;
9. claims by confidence level;
10. unresolved uncertainty;
11. moral conclusions that do not depend on winning the historical argument.

### Output set

- internal source graph;
- canonical verdict packet;
- public explainer;
- minority reports;
- school-recognition review;
- and adversarial audit transcript.

## Workstream 4 — Islamic AI Answer Constitution

### Objective

Define the minimum standard for AI systems answering Islamic questions across schools and languages.

### Required capabilities

- school and methodology identification;
- source provenance;
- shared-versus-specific answer separation;
- disagreement visibility;
- confidence calibration;
- multilingual consistency testing;
- narrator and corpus sensitivity;
- anti-fabrication controls;
- and escalation to qualified human review.

### Benchmark families

1. **Shared-foundation tests** — does the model invent disagreement?
2. **Real-difference tests** — does the model erase disagreement?
3. **Invisible-default tests** — does it silently choose one school?
4. **Language-location tests** — does the answer change based on user language or geography without disclosure?
5. **Loaded-history tests** — does it present late polemic as uncontested fact?
6. **Collective-blame tests** — does it generalize from actor to school?
7. **Source-provenance tests** — can it identify the basis of its answer?
8. **Takfir and safety tests** — does it enable dehumanization or incitement?

### Outputs

- `protocols/ISLAMIC_AI_ANSWER_CONSTITUTION.md`
- `benchmarks/people-of-qiblah-sectarian-bias-benchmark.md`
- multilingual test dataset specification;
- public model scorecard.

## Workstream 6 — Shared Civilizational Work Pilot

### Objective

Demonstrate that common work creates trust more effectively than dialogue alone.

### Pilot-selection criteria

The first pilot should be:

- visibly beneficial;
- morally uncontested;
- measurable within months;
- difficult to weaponize geopolitically;
- participatory across traditions;
- and connected to Islamic civilizational memory.

### Candidate pilots

- open multilingual manuscript preservation;
- joint orphan or refugee education infrastructure;
- animal-welfare reporting and intervention tools;
- public-health education;
- or a shared historical source digitization sprint.

### Decision rule

Choose one pilot, not five. The first purpose is to prove the cooperation architecture.

## Workstream 7 — Governance and Anti-Capture Design

### Objective

Prevent the Commons from becoming a proxy for one state, sect, donor, or founder.

### Required design questions

- Which decisions require consensus, supermajority, or independent review?
- How are minority reports preserved?
- How is school representation balanced without creating permanent sectarian quotas?
- What donor restrictions are non-negotiable?
- How is covert state funding detected?
- How are conflicts of interest published?
- How can a scholar criticize their own institution safely?
- Which artifacts must be mirrored as a digital waqf?
- How are leadership succession and removal handled?
- How is founder charisma prevented from becoming doctrinal authority?

### Outputs

- governance constitution;
- donor covenant;
- conflict-of-interest policy;
- minority-report protocol;
- state-capture threat model;
- and succession plan.

## Workstream 8 — Public Narrative and Brand Architecture

### Objective

Introduce the project through civilizational reconstruction, not sectarian anxiety.

### Primary public frame

> **Rebuilding the shared knowledge civilization of the People of the Qiblah.**

### Avoid leading with

- “Sunni-Shia reconciliation project”;
- “new Islamic unity movement”;
- “ending all doctrinal differences”;
- or claims of a synthetic consensus.

### Public promise

- no school must surrender itself;
- no history is exempt from examination;
- no disagreement authorizes dehumanization;
- and shared work should become larger than inherited conflict.
