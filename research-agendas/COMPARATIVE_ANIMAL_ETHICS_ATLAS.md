# Comparative Animal Ethics Atlas

**Status:** Research agenda  
**Canonical benchmark:** `canon/ANIMAL_CIVILIZATION_BENCHMARK.md`

## Objective

Build the first repository-grade comparative atlas of how civilizations understood, used, protected, neglected, symbolized, and institutionalized obligations toward animals.

The atlas should not rank civilizations by inspirational quotations. It should compare equivalent layers:

`ontology → norm → law → institution → enforcement → practice → succession → correction`

## Core research thesis

Animal treatment is a high-leverage proxy for moral restraint under power asymmetry. It should become a flagship dimension in the Civilizational Intelligence Benchmark and in the repository's expanded definition of a moral Golden Age.

## Scoring rules

- Score dimensions, not civilizations globally.
- Separate elite text from mass practice.
- Separate sacred species from universal concern.
- Separate reduced killing from reduced suffering.
- Separate symbolic personification from legal protection.
- Separate individual saints/philosophers from representative institutions.
- Compare similar periods, technologies, economies, and animal uses.
- Publish confidence and missing evidence alongside every score.

## Flagship research questions

1. Was the Qur'anic view of animals unusually non-anthropocentric for the late antique Near East?
2. Which animal rules were inherited from Jewish, Byzantine, Persian, Arab, or Indian traditions?
3. Did Islamic law create duties directly owed to animals, or mainly duties owed to God/owners/human moral character?
4. Where did fiqh constrain profitable cruelty?
5. Did market inspectors actually enforce animal-welfare norms?
6. Were there durable animal-focused endowments, or mostly later folklore and isolated cases?
7. Did urban Islamic societies treat dogs, cats, birds, horses, camels, livestock, and wildlife differently?
8. How did war, famine, empire, and commercial expansion change the norms?
9. Were Muslim societies morally ahead of their Mediterranean contemporaries but behind Jain/Buddhist standards?
10. Which dimensions of Islamic animal ethics could be revived under modern factory farming and ecological crisis?
