## Master Thesis

> **Every civilization leaves fingerprints.**

The Mīzān Archive is a civilizational mystery universe in which investigators reconstruct contested history from revelation, chronology, manuscripts, testimony, material evidence, authority networks, incentives, institutions, and historical fruit.

Every story begins as a mystery, becomes a trial of memory, and ends as a civilizational design question.

The universe is designed to function simultaneously as:

- an original narrative franchise;
- a public historical-intelligence interface;
- a propaganda-literacy school;
- an entry point into Islamic civilizational history;
- an interactive evidence system;
- an AI-agent architecture;
- a bridge from memory to real institution-building.

## Canon Rule

The fiction may reveal, teach, dramatize, and compress.

It may not:

- invent historical evidence;
- silently erase uncertainty;
- confuse Qur'an with hadith, jurisprudence, empire, or culture;
- convert a dramatic scene into a historical claim without a source ledger;
- depict sacred persons or texts disrespectfully;
- reduce Muslim history to apologetics or enemies to caricatures.

Every historical claim presented as fact must be traceable to the truth infrastructure beneath the story.
