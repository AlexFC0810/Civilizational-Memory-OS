## I · THE SEED — the moral kernel (Qur'anic & Prophetic norms)

**LB-01** · [T1] The Qur'an's exclusive-absoluteness claim (tawhid) is explicitly deployed against human absolutism — Pharaoh's "I am your lord, the most high" as the archetype of damnation (79:24), priests-as-lords condemned (9:31), obedience bounded by the ma'ruf limit in sahih hadith (Bukhari 7144, 7257) — and a body of scholarship reads this as a standing self-criticism vocabulary: a settlement won through contest, not a founding given. The same card carries the breakers inside it: "shadow of God" royal titles, the quietist strand, Khawarij-to-hakimiyya dual-use, and the Mihna. — **Carries:** the causal root of ruler-accountability (LB-09), the Mihna's meaning (LB-41), and the self-correction thesis — carded with the dual-use and practice-gap breakers built in. — *Trace:* CMOS-0011 · B · synthesis (L1/L2 established; function claim probable).

**LB-02** · [T1] The Qur'an condemns the moral world in which baby girls could be buried alive by giving the buried girl a voice on the Day of Judgment. — **Carries:** the moral inversion at Islam's origin — the powerless given standing against the powerful; breaks "Islam degraded women" at the source layer. — *Trace:* CMOS-0002 · A · quran.

**LB-03** · [T1] The Qur'an gave women defined financial rights — dowry, inheritance shares, protection from being inherited against their will — though Muslim societies often fell short of the norm; under English coverture, by contrast, a married woman's independent property identity was restricted into the 19th century. — **Carries:** the source-vs-practice distinction AND the comparative timeline that reframes "Islam vs women's rights"; the built-in caveat models the house's honesty standard. — *Trace:* CMOS-0001 · A (+ coverture: PROPHETIC_INVERSION #3 · B) · quran/outcome.

**LB-04** · [T2] Qur'an 49:13 recognizes peoples and tribes but makes righteousness, not lineage, the measure of nobility — even though Muslim societies have had ethnic hierarchies and racism in practice. — **Carries:** the anti-tribal operating principle that made a portable, multi-ethnic civilization possible; carries its own adherence-gap caveat. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #3; PROPHETIC_INVERSION #8 · A · quran.

**LB-05** · [T1] The Qur'an's individual-accountability principle ("no bearer of burdens bears another's burden") makes collective guilt sit badly with both moral logic and the text — and the populations hit hardest by jihadist terrorism are themselves majority-Muslim. — **Carries:** the load-bearing answer to the era's most consequential smear, with its empirical leg honestly scoped. — *Trace:* CMOS-0007 · A · quran/outcome.

**LB-06** · [T2] Islam places wealth under moral accountability: the poor, needy, debtor, traveler, captive, and orphan are central concerns of the moral-legal order, not objects of optional charity. — **Carries:** the seed of the entire welfare architecture (zakat → waqf → bimaristan); mercy as obligation, not sentiment. — *Trace:* PROPHETIC_INVERSION #11 · A · quran.

**LB-07** · [T1] The Prophetic tradition makes treatment of powerless creatures a measure of the soul: punishment for starving a cat, forgiveness for watering a thirsty dog, reward for serving every living being, ihsan even in slaughter, prohibition on making living beings targets. — **Carries:** the moral-circle radius claim — ethics extending below the human line centuries before modern animal-welfare law; five Tier-A hadith anchors. — *Trace:* CMOS-0003 · A; AMH-001…005 ledger · Tier A · prophetic.

**LB-08** · [T2] Sahih al-Bukhari opens on the hadith that deeds depend upon intentions. — **Carries:** interiority as the root of the whole ethical system — law and ritual downstream of conscience; explains adab, ihsan, and the moral-psychology layer. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #1 · A · prophetic.

**LB-09** · [T2] Authenticated hadith rule that there is "no obedience for evil deeds" — a Muslim need not obey an unlawful command from a ruler. — **Carries:** the conscience-clause against absolutism; the seed of ruler-accountability and the sacred right of refusal. — *Trace:* ruler-accountability case · Established · prophetic.

**LB-10** · [T2] Qur'an 2:190 sets a non-aggression and proportionality limit on war, and early Islam articulated an unusually explicit, religiously binding code of restraint — more developed as enforceable doctrine than most 7th-century baselines — which Muslim armies nonetheless repeatedly violated in practice. — **Carries:** the rules-of-war map with both halves load-bearing: the advanced ideal AND the uneven reality. — *Trace:* rules-of-war case (grade A) · Established · quran/fiqh.

**LB-11** · [T1] The first Islamic polity in Medina was a negotiated, plural covenant — worship, welfare, law, defense, and intergroup agreements — that did not require religious or ethnic sameness to begin, though the textual history of the "Constitution of Medina" requires scholarly caveats. — **Carries:** plurality present at the founding moment, not imported later; the covenant-above-tribe mechanism. — *Trace:* **CMOS-0016** · B · prophetic (carded 2026-07-20; named parties Muslims/Jewish clans/pagan Arabs — NOT Christians; transmission via Ibn Ishaq→Ibn Hisham stated as report-not-archive; later tribal exile/execution conceded).

**LB-12** · [T2] Islam contains a strong manumission architecture — freeing the enslaved as moral good, a zakat category for liberation (9:60), manumission in expiation rules, contractual emancipation (24:33) — but historical Muslim societies did not complete an abolitionary transition: "abolitionary vector" is accurate; "completed abolition" and "pro-slavery" are both false. — **Carries:** the honest frame for the hardest institutional question; both the architecture and the failure are load-bearing. — *Trace:* islam-slavery-manumission ledger §10 · High confidence · quran/fiqh/outcome.

**LB-13** · [T1] The Qur'an repeatedly narrates civilizations that grew wealthy, powerful, and morally numb — and fell — functioning as built-in threat-detection against the assumption that success proves righteousness. — **Carries:** the civilization's internal decline-theory; the reason self-criticism is a religious act, not disloyalty. — *Trace:* **CMOS-0018** · B · quran (carded 2026-07-20; 21 verses verbatim from quran.com — 79:24 + 22:45 CoS-spot-verified; A-strength textual pattern / B interpretive function; non-uniqueness + adherence-gap + triumphal-register conceded).

## II · THE SYSTEM — law and institutions

**LB-14** · [T1] Sharia is the divinely guided path at source level; fiqh is human interpretation; state law is codification; culture is inherited practice — and punishments exist in Islamic law without being the center of the moral-legal ecology. — **Carries:** the category firewall itself — the four-layer separation every honest claim about "Islamic law" depends on. — *Trace:* CMOS-0004 · A · fiqh.

**LB-15** · [T2] The waqf — a perpetual, inalienable endowment — was the major legal-financial mechanism converting private wealth into durable public goods for roughly a millennium; much of its capital also flowed to founders' families, and its rigidity is argued to have constrained later economic adaptation. — **Carries:** the funding engine of the golden ages AND a leading decline hypothesis in one institution; the reimplementation target (digital waqf). — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #8 · A · institution.

**LB-16** · [T2] From the late 8th to 14th century the bimaristan became a durable endowment-funded institution — free inpatient treatment, specialized wards, clinical teaching, care for the mentally ill — inheriting rather than inventing the hospital from Byzantine and Sasanian precedents. — **Carries:** compressed proof that faith → charity → endowment → institution → compounding knowledge; the "inherited, then scaled" honesty beats "invented" and survives hostile review. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #9 · A · institution.

**LB-17** · [T2] From its first centuries, Islamic scholarship ran a documented provenance system — isnad and ijaza — making knowledge traceable to a named human chain. — **Carries:** the civilization's information-integrity protocol; an ancestor-in-spirit of citation and peer review (the analogy itself is graded Contested/B — never inflate it into a priority claim) and the direct ancestor of this repo's own verification transcripts. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #19 · A · knowledge.

**LB-18** · [T2] The Qur'anic norm of honest measure was institutionalized over centuries into the muhtasib — a publicly appointed market inspector exercising hisbah — via a staged development from Prophetic-era market oversight to a titled Abbasid office. — **Carries:** scripture → institution causal chain made visible in one office; consumer protection as religious infrastructure. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #16 · A · institution.

**LB-19** · [T1] Dhimmi status was a structured, unequal legal category that — compared to several same-era alternatives — more often than not functioned as survivable, taxable minority status rather than forced-conversion-or-death, though enforcement varied and sometimes escalated into forced conversion or massacre (Granada 1066; the Almohads). — **Carries:** the calibrated tolerance claim: real inequality AND comparative survivability, with the civilization's own worst episodes named inside the claim. — *Trace:* CMOS-0008 · A · outcome.

**LB-20** · [T2] Maqasid al-Shariah — the higher objectives of the law — classically include preservation of religion, life, intellect, lineage, and property. — **Carries:** the purposive layer that makes "Islamic law = rules" a category error; the bridge from jurisprudence to ethics. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #12 · B · fiqh.

**LB-21** · [T2] The Qur'an commands protection of orphans' property with witnesses and no self-dealing, framing misappropriation as damnable — with uneven enforcement across later societies. — **Carries:** fiduciary duty as sacred obligation; the micro-scale proof of wealth-under-accountability. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #17 · A · quran.

**LB-22** · [T1] Classical Islamic law was more decentralized than the modern word "law" suggests — normative authority distributed among jurists, judges, rulers, and communities — so the civilization could lose political unity without losing legal and scholarly coherence. — **Carries:** the resilience mechanism; why the map survived the empire. — *Trace:* **CMOS-0015** · B · fiqh (carded 2026-07-20; resilience clause firewalled as probable inference carried by LB-28).

## III · THE FRUIT — knowledge and outcomes

**LB-23** · [T1] Across roughly the 8th–14th centuries, rulers and elites built durable, endowment-funded institutions for knowledge and healing — a sustained translation movement, waqf-funded bimaristans, named scholars whose work fed into European science through documented transmission chains. — **Carries:** the anchor institutional claim of the golden ages, gate-passed with live-fetched anchors. — *Trace:* CMOS-0005 · A · institution.

**LB-24** · [T2] Islamic civilization did not merely preserve ancient knowledge; it translated, commented, synthesized, expanded, institutionalized, and transmitted it — preservation was the floor, transformation the achievement. — **Carries:** kills the "just copied the Greeks" smear and the passive-librarian myth in one move. — *Trace:* GOLDEN_AGES ledger Claim 2 · B ("likely A with source package") · knowledge.

**LB-25** · [T2] In early 11th-century Cairo, Ibn al-Haytham argued the intromission theory of vision and insisted claims be tested by systematic controlled experiment combined with mathematical proof. — **Carries:** experimental method as practice inside the civilization — with the "first" superlative honestly contested (the Schramm-vs-Toomer dispute, documented in CMOS-0005's Evidence Grade notes) rather than asserted. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #18 · A · knowledge.

**LB-26** · [T2] Religious obligations — prayer times, qibla, inheritance shares, calendars — generated recurring technical demand for astronomy, geometry, arithmetic, and law. — **Carries:** the causal arrow from worship to science; devotion as R&D driver, not obstacle. — *Trace:* GOLDEN_AGES ledger Claim 4 ("Sacred precision") · B* · knowledge.

**LB-27** · [T2] There was no single Islamic Golden Age: plural, overlapping flowerings across regions, centuries, dynasties, and disciplines. — **Carries:** replaces the fragile one-age-then-collapse timeline with the rolling-arc map; prerequisite for every decline claim. — *Trace:* GOLDEN_AGES ledger Claim 1 · B; Byzantium ledger IGA-001 · Probable · culture.

**LB-28** · [T2] Baghdad was a major but never sole center, and Islamic civilization renewed and peaked after 1258 — Ilkhanid, Timurid, Mamluk, Ottoman, Safavid, Mughal, West African, Indian Ocean. — **Carries:** breaks "the Mongols ended it"; the network-not-capital topology of the civilization. — *Trace:* Byzantium ledger IGA-002/IGA-004 · Established · outcome.

**LB-29** · [T1] Construction of the Maragha observatory began in 1259 — one year after the sack of Baghdad — under the patronage of Hülegü himself, the non-Muslim conqueror who had destroyed Baghdad, funded from the conquered civilization's own waqf revenue and directed by al-Tusi; the observatory itself was inactive by the early 14th century. — **Carries:** the sharpest single empirical emblem of LB-28 — one node re-anchored within a year, on the conqueror's own court; the network-survival generalization stays LB-28's load, not this date's. — *Trace:* CMOS-0010 · B · institution (founding established; resilience inference probable, carried by LB-28).

**LB-30** · [T1] Islamic civilization's intellectual infrastructure was multi-confessional — Christian, Jewish, Sabian, Zoroastrian-descended scholars as genuine contributors. — **Carries:** the knowledge system's absorption principle ("truth does not become foreign because its previous speaker was foreign"); bridge-relevant and smear-breaking at once. — *Trace:* **CMOS-0017** · B · culture (carded 2026-07-20; Hunayn/Bukhtishu/Thabit ibn Qurra/Maimonides anchored; genuine contributors AND legally-subordinate dhimmis both true — al-Mutawakkil 850 + Almohad exile conceded; Zoroastrian strand honestly = converts not practicing-dhimmi).

**LB-31** · [T2-D] Europe encountered an Arabic intellectual world, not merely rediscovered Greek texts — Avicenna and Averroes had already reorganized and transformed Greek thought before Latin translation. — **Carries:** corrects the "Renaissance = Greek revival" map at its hinge; the transmission story's true shape. — *Trace:* seed thesis 17 · D · knowledge. **CARDING PRIORITY #7.**

**LB-32** · [T2] Muslim rule existed in parts of Iberia from 711 to 1492 — but not all of Spain, continuously. — **Carries:** the precision that keeps every al-Andalus claim honest; small fact, large calibration duty. — *Trace:* religion-secularism audit #6 · qualified · outcome.

**LB-33** · [T2] Tarif Khalidi documented roughly 300 sayings and stories of Jesus across Islamic literature from the 8th–18th centuries — a millennium-long "Muslim gospel" of reverence. — **Carries:** the bridge fact: reverence for Jesus as a continuous literary tradition, not a modern talking point. — *Trace:* jesus-mary bridge case (grade A) · Established · culture.

**LB-34** · [T2] The Qur'an honours Jesus as Messiah and miracle-worker and Mary as chosen and purified — the only woman with a Qur'anic chapter in her name — while the same texts deny Jesus's divinity, sonship, and crucifixion-death (4:157). — **Carries:** the whole islamlovesjesus.org thesis with the differences kept in, not papered over — the bridge that doesn't collapse under theological scrutiny. — *Trace:* jesus-mary bridge case · Established (grade A case) · quran.

## V · CALIBRATION — the breaker anchors (the map is weaker without them)

**LB-40** · [T2] After the Banu Qurayza's surrender (c. 627), the adult men were executed — in numbers variously reported between roughly 400 and 900 — and women and children enslaved. — **Carries:** the hardest Prophetic-era fact, held openly; the credibility of every defensive claim in this archive depends on this entry existing. — *Trace:* rules-of-war case (grade A) · Established (event) / Contested (scale) · prophetic.

**LB-41** · [T2] During the Mihna (833–c.851), Abbasid caliphs coerced scholars to affirm state theology, imprisoning and flogging dissenters including Ahmad ibn Hanbal. — **Carries:** the ideal of ruler-under-law violated from inside — and the scholars' resistance as the system's antibody; proof the map includes its own failures. — *Trace:* ruler-accountability case · Established · institution.

**LB-42** · [T2] Muslim societies continued practicing slavery for centuries despite the manumission architecture — with both humane integration (Mamluk mobility) and severe exploitation (Zanj labor) in the record. — **Carries:** the adherence gap at its widest; pairs with LB-12 so neither apologetics nor smear survives contact. — *Trace:* islam-slavery-manumission ledger §10 · High confidence · outcome.

**LB-43** · [T1] Byzantium ran endowed hospitals before and alongside the bimaristans — the 1136 Pantokrator Typikon specifies a ~50-bed, five-ward hospital with salaried physicians, and it was not the first Byzantine hospital. — **Carries:** the comparative anchor that keeps LB-16/LB-23 honest; crediting the precedent is what makes the Islamic case trustworthy. — *Trace:* CMOS-0009 · B · provenance-audited · institution.

**LB-44** · [T2] The House of Wisdom is associated with Abbasid Baghdad's library and translation culture, but popular narratives may overstate its institutional form. — **Carries:** the myth-calibration duty on our own side's favorite symbol. — *Trace:* ISLAMIC_CIV_OS ledger #10 · B/C · institution.

**LB-45** · [T2] The Aisha marital-age report is traditional and canonical within much Sunni hadith; modern source-critical scholarship (Little) questions its reliability; and even accepting it, the clinical smear overreaches the evidence. — **Carries:** the blast-radius topic's honest state — canonical-but-contested — which is the only position that survives both directions of bad faith. **LB-46** · [T1] Muslim political conquest expanded in decades; conversion of conquered populations unfolded over centuries through trade, marriage, and opportunity — so neither "spread by the sword alone" nor "power played no role" is accurate. — **Carries:** the conquest/conversion distinction that defuses an entire smear family. — *Trace:* **CMOS-0014** · B · outcome (carded 2026-07-20; conquest-speed sub-claim A-strength, multi-causal conversion synthesis B on Bulliet's curve; real coercion conceded).

**LB-47** · [T2] The 2004–05 Amman Message — endorsed by 500+ scholars — recognized eight legal schools across Sunni, Shia, and Ibadi Islam, prohibited takfir among them, and set fatwa preconditions. — **Carries:** documented, living cross-school consensus; the strongest single anti-sectarian and anti-extremist institutional fact. — *Trace:* ahl-al-qiblah + qiblah-momentum ledgers · Established · institution.

**LB-48** · [T2] "People of the Qiblah" is a classical legal-theological category — al-Tahawi's creed declares those who pray toward the qiblah Muslims and rejects takfir for sin alone. — **Carries:** intra-Muslim inclusion as a *classical* norm, not a modern invention; the deep root under LB-47. — *Trace:* ahl-al-qiblah ledger §2 · Strong primary-text lead (edition verification pending) · fiqh.
