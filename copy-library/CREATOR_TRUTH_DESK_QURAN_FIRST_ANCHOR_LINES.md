# 1. The Source Constitution

## Full opening

> “Before we debate fourteen centuries, let’s agree on what counts as evidence. For Muslims, the Qur’an is the verbatim revelation and the highest authority. The Prophet’s Sunnah is his lived model of that revelation. Hadith are later human reports used to reconstruct that model, and they vary in reliability. Fiqh is human jurisprudence. Empires, governments, movements, and individual Muslims are implementations—not God speaking.
>
> “So every time either of us says ‘Islam says,’ we identify the layer. Is it the Qur’an? The Prophet’s actual example? A hadith report? A jurist? A medieval empire? A modern government? A criminal? Then we prove the chain and the inference.
>
> “A priest abusing a child is not Jesus teaching Christianity. But a church that protects him can still be judged for institutional corruption. The same standard applies to Muslims: a ruler’s crime is not automatically Islam, but no Muslim institution gets immunity if it justifies or conceals wrongdoing. Quote the source. Name the layer. Then we can debate honestly.”

## Seven seconds
