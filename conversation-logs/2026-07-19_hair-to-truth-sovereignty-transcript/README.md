## Preservation standard

This directory distinguishes three artifact types:

1. **Verbatim public dialogue** — exact user- and assistant-visible text still recoverable in the active conversation context.
2. **Recovery summary — not verbatim** — a clearly marked reconstruction of earlier public turns that had already been compacted by the platform before archival.
3. **Canonization receipt** — the durable doctrine, files, pull requests, and evals produced from the exchange.

This archive does **not** contain:

- private chain-of-thought;
- hidden model reasoning;
- internal safety deliberation;
- tool payloads or connector internals;
- platform metadata unrelated to the substantive exchange.

No missing wording is fabricated. Any gap created by context compaction is labeled as a recovery summary. A future exact platform export may replace or supplement those gaps while preserving this version as provenance.

## Why the path matters

The final output alone does not preserve:

- the initial model’s assumptions;
- the operator’s anomaly detection;
- the repeated pushback against Western-source monoculture and bureaucratic consensus;
- the distinction between first principles and a complete epistemology;
- the discovery that mechanisms, outcomes, traditions, testimony, and N-of-1 evidence require triangulation;
- the later discovery that truth can remain factually cautious while being morally falsified through sanitization;
- the transition from one-answer correction to system-level doctrine.

The conversation is therefore treated as a **model-improvement training case**, not disposable chat.
