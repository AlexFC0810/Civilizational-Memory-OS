# Evidence cards — Mercy as law — the powerless given standing

Each section below is a gate-verified evidence card (deploy-facing sections only). Grades: A = strong primary/consensus support; B = strong secondary support. Treat caveats as part of the claim, not hedging to skip.

## CMOS-0002 — evidence grade A (A: Qur'an 81:8-9 condemnation of infanticide; B/C: pre-Islamic prevalence)
*Source closure: reconnaissance · layer: quran*

### Best Load-Bearing Question
> **If Islam hates women, why does the Qur'an give the buried baby girl the courtroom of Judgment Day?**

### Alternate Cold-Friendly Question
> **In a world that could bury daughters, why did the Qur'an make the buried girl testify against the society that killed her?**

### S-Tier Lines
> **In a world that buried daughters, the Qur'an made the buried girl testify against civilization.**

> **The Qur'an did not bury the daughter; it resurrected her as evidence against injustice.**

> **The unwanted daughter became sacred evidence against the society that discarded her.**

### Best Public Response
> “The claim that Islam hates girls collapses when you read Qur'an 81:8-9. The Qur'an imagines the buried baby girl being asked for what crime she was killed. The point is not that every pre-Islamic Arab family buried daughters; the point is that the Qur'an morally indicts a world where a daughter could be treated as disposable.”

### Short Public Response
> “If Islam hates women, why does the Qur'an give the buried baby girl the courtroom of Judgment Day?”

### Content Angles


### Honest Caveats
1. Do not say all Arabs buried their daughters before Islam.
2. Do not use this as proof that all Muslim societies later treated girls well.
3. Do not claim Islam was the first or only tradition to condemn female infanticide unless separately sourced.
4. Do not overstate historical prevalence without academic backing.
5. Keep the claim focused on the Qur'anic moral inversion.

### Safe Wording
> The Qur'an condemns the moral world in which baby girls could be buried alive by giving the buried girl a voice on the Day of Judgment.

## CMOS-0003 — evidence grade A (A: sahih hadith mercy anchors; B: broader civilizational animal-welfare norms)
*Source closure: reconnaissance · layer: prophetic*

### Best Load-Bearing Question
> **If Islam is only harshness, why can cruelty to a cat damn someone and mercy to a dog help save someone?**

### Alternate Cold-Friendly Question
> **What kind of “barbaric” religion teaches that a thirsty dog can become the reason God forgives someone?**

### S-Tier Lines
> **In Islam, cruelty to a cat can damn a person, and mercy to a dog can help save one.**

> **Prophetic mercy was not sentimental. It regulated how power treats the powerless — even animals.**

> **Islamic mercy is not only how you treat people. It reaches the creature that cannot speak for itself.**

### Best Public Response
> “If Islam is only harshness, the animal mercy narrations are hard to explain. A woman is punished for imprisoning a cat until it dies. A person is forgiven for giving water to a thirsty dog. The Prophet ﷺ says there is reward in serving every living being. Even slaughter is regulated by ihsan. Muslims have not always lived up to this, but the source-level architecture is not barbarism.”

### Short Public Response
> “If Islam is only harshness, why can cruelty to a cat damn someone and mercy to a dog help save someone?”

### Content Angles


### Honest Caveats
1. Do not claim Muslims have always treated animals well.
2. Do not claim Islam is the only tradition with animal mercy.
3. Do not use animal mercy to dodge hard questions about slaughter, meat, hunting, or legal debates.
4. Do not imply the hadith abolish animal use; they regulate power through mercy and accountability.
5. Do not overstate “animal rights” in a modern legal sense unless defining terms carefully.

### Safe Wording
> The Prophetic tradition contains concrete teachings of animal mercy, including punishment for starving a cat, forgiveness for giving water to a thirsty dog, reward for serving living beings, and ihsan even in slaughter.

## CMOS-0001 — evidence grade A (A: Qur'anic property/dowry/inheritance norms; B: coverture comparison)
*Source closure: reconnaissance · layer: quran*

### Best Load-Bearing Question
> **If Muslim women were simply property, why did the Qur'an give women inheritance, dowry, and moral agency before God?**

### S-Tier Lines
> **Gender distinction is not the same thing as female erasure.**

> **The Qur'an did not treat women as property; it gave women property.**

> **Muslim societies often fell short. But the source-level claim that Islam gave women no rights collapses under the Qur'an itself.**

### Best Public Response
> “Muslim societies have often failed women, and some failures were justified with religious language. But the claim that Islam simply treated women as property does not survive the Qur'an. The Qur'an gave women dowry, inheritance shares, and direct moral accountability before God, and it forbade inheriting women against their will. So the better question is: are we judging Islam by its sources, or by cultures that Islam itself came to discipline?”

### Short Public Response
> “If Muslim women were simply property, why did the Qur'an give women inheritance, dowry, and moral agency before God?”

### Content Angles


### Honest Caveats
1. Do not claim Muslim societies always implemented these rights justly.
2. Do not claim Islam created modern liberal gender equality.
3. Do not ignore contested issues in Islamic family law.
4. Do not overuse coverture as if it represents every Western society or all of Western history.
5. Do not say “Islam invented women's rights.” That is too broad and fragile.
6. Do not imply women always receive equal inheritance shares; Islamic inheritance is structured by kinship role and financial duty, not identical shares in every case.

### Safe Wording
> The Qur'an gave women defined financial rights, including dowry and inheritance shares, and forbade inheriting women against their will. Muslim societies often fell short, but the source-level claim that Islam gave women no rights is false.
