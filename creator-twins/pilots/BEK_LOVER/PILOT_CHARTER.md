# Bek Lover Epistemic Twin Pilot Charter

**Status:** Ready for first transcript intake  
**Pilot:** Creator Epistemic Twin + Creator Supercharger + Council of Minds  
**Name spelling:** Bek Lover

## Purpose

Use Bek Lover's public long-form corpus as the first full dogfood run for the Creator Epistemic Twin architecture.

The purpose is not to build a personality clone or an AI that deceptively speaks as Bek Lover.

The purpose is to create a high-fidelity, provenance-linked model of his expressed intellectual work that can:

- preserve his recurring questions and distinctions;
- reconstruct his strongest causal and historical synthesis;
- verify factual claims and cited evidence;
- surface contradictions, evolution, and uncertainty;
- identify what he notices that generic AI routinely misses;
- extend his work with clearly labeled AIOS augmentation;
- support better podcasts, interviews, debates, research, clips, writing, and collaborations;
- compare his map with complementary thinkers through the Council of Minds.

> **The pilot succeeds when the corpus becomes more useful, truthful, connected, and generative than the original archive—without falsely attributing the augmentation to the creator.**

## First intake event

The first artifact will be a user-uploaded transcript of a Bek Lover video.

Upon upload, the AIOS should immediately run:

1. source metadata capture;
2. transcript normalization;
3. speaker and segment mapping;
4. atomic claim extraction;
5. claim-family assignment;
6. recurring-question extraction;
7. causal-model extraction;
8. narrative and moral-grammar extraction;
9. source and factual audit;
10. unique-insight detection;
11. augmentation opportunities;
12. creator-supercharge outputs;
13. next-corpus recommendation;
14. GitHub integration.

## Six-mode boundary

Every output must remain visibly separated.

### Archive Mode

What Bek Lover actually said, with source, timestamp, context, and transcription confidence.

### Reconstruction Mode

The strongest coherent account of the view supported by the transcript or larger corpus.

### Audit Mode

What survives source verification, causal scrutiny, and adversarial review.

### Augmentation Mode

Connections, evidence, frameworks, questions, and implications added by the AIOS. These must never be presented as Bek Lover's existing belief.

### Creator-Approved Mode

Material explicitly corrected, affirmed, or approved by Bek Lover if he later participates.

### Council Mode

What emerges when the Bek Lover Twin is compared with another high-fidelity mind model.

## Corpus-scale target

After the first transcript proves the workflow, expand toward:

1. **Source manifest** — all accessible public podcasts, interviews, debates, videos, articles, posts, and lectures.
2. **Normalized transcript corpus** — machine-searchable with episode, speaker, date, and timestamp metadata.
3. **Top-100 claim ledger** — the highest-value recurring factual, causal, moral, and predictive claims.
4. **Question Genome profile** — the questions and anomalies he repeatedly uses to organize inquiry.
5. **Belief and causal graph** — the relationships among events, institutions, actors, incentives, and outcomes.
6. **Narrative spine** — his broad story of history, civilization, power, media, and morality.
7. **Source hierarchy** — recurring authorities, books, historians, witnesses, and evidentiary preferences.
8. **Intellectual-evolution timeline** — changes, reversals, refinements, and unresolved tensions.
9. **Contradiction ledger** — genuine inconsistencies, contextual differences, or outdated positions.
10. **Verification layer** — accepted, probable, contested, unsupported, and superseded claims.
11. **Compression library** — strongest stories, metaphors, distinctions, and public lines.
12. **Frontier map** — questions he has raised but not yet resolved.
13. **Opportunity graph** — podcasts, guests, collaborations, series, books, documentaries, and institutions.

## Council-of-Minds experiment

The first Council comparison should select a thinker who is:

- complementary rather than merely similar;
- strong on an adjacent layer;
- independently sourced;
- capable of revealing convergence, contradiction, or a missing causal mechanism.

The output must preserve:

- what Bek uniquely notices;
- what the second thinker uniquely notices;
- where both rely on the same source chain;
- where their models genuinely conflict;
- what evidence could adjudicate;
- what synthesis emerges only in combination.

## Source and identity safeguards

- Do not publish synthetic first-person Bek statements without explicit approval.
- Never represent an inference as a quotation.
- Preserve dates and intellectual evolution.
- Do not manufacture consistency.
- Do not scrape private or restricted material.
- Distinguish public-corpus reconstruction from creator-approved representation.
- Do not clone voice, image, or identity without explicit authorization.
- Allow the audit to conclude that a claim is wrong.
- Allow the creator to reject an AIOS augmentation.
- Preserve the creator's authentic voice rather than replacing it with institutional prose.

## Pilot success criteria

The first transcript run succeeds if it:

- faithfully reconstructs the central argument;
- identifies at least one non-obvious question or causal connection;
- catches factual or source weaknesses without flattening the overall insight;
- produces at least one genuinely useful creator-facing asset;
- reveals improvements needed in the Epistemic Twin protocol;
- makes the second transcript easier to ingest and integrate;
- leaves full provenance in GitHub.

The corpus pilot succeeds if:

- Bek or a knowledgeable reviewer judges the reconstruction high-fidelity;
- preparation time falls materially;
- forgotten insights are recovered;
- source errors are reduced;
- new synthesis is produced;
- at least one real podcast, collaboration, series, or intellectual product improves because of the system;
- every use makes the Twin and AIOS better.
