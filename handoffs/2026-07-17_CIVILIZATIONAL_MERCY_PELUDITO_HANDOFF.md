## 3. Canonical definitions

### Civilizational Mercy

The capacity of a civilization to preserve moral visibility and enforce responsibility toward beings beneath concentrated human power, especially when they cannot compel protection.

### Voiceless Covenant

> A being’s inability to speak, vote, testify, own property, organize, or retaliate does not weaken its claim against cruelty. It strengthens our duty of protection.

### Civilizational Mercy Stack

Ontology → Story → Ritual → Conscience → Law → Institution → Visibility → Intervention → Accountability → Memory.

### Distance–Innocence Machine

A system that separates beneficiary, decision-maker, operator, and victim until participation in harm feels authorless.

### Digital Amphitheater

A network in which the device becomes witness, stage, status mechanism, distribution system, and potential market for cruelty.

### Mercy formula

> ʿAsabiyyah supplies collective power. Raḥmah determines whom it protects. Sharīʿah constrains what it may do. Taqwā restrains it when nobody is watching.

### Bismillah Test

> Can this act honestly be begun in the name of the Most Merciful?

This is not a substitute for law or evidence. It is a moral formation heuristic for testing whether power is being exercised with legitimate purpose, proportionality, competence, restraint, and minimized suffering.

- Do not describe entire societies as spiritually empty.
- No “Islam invented animal mercy” claim.

- No false equivalence between animal harm and genocide; use structural comparison carefully.

## 10. Quality bar

A deliverable is ready only when it is:

- emotionally powerful without exploiting suffering;
- accurate under hostile scrutiny;
- useful across cultures;
- clear to a nonexpert;
- actionable;
- measurable;
- resistant to political, religious, and engagement capture;
- capable of protecting a future animal rather than merely memorializing a past victim;
- explicit about whether it is universal education, Islamic explanation, or Muslim self-audit;
- reviewed by the relevant linguistic, theological, legal, child-safety, and welfare expertise.
