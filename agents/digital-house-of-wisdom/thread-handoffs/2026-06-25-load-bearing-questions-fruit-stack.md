## Core Unlocks

### 1. Load-Bearing Questions

A load-bearing question is compressed truth with a question mark.

It should be:

- cold-friendly;
- concrete;
- morally serious;
- truthful under pressure;
- memorable;
- strong enough to force clarification.

Canonical line:

> A slogan is not a source. A smear is not an argument.

Example:

> If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim?

### 2. Cold-Friendly Standard

A line is not S-tier unless a cold audience can understand it without insider context.

Bad:

> Maybe Islam is incompatible with modernity's diseases.

Better:

> Maybe Islam is not incompatible with addiction, loneliness, pornography, debt, family collapse, alcohol abuse, gambling, and worship of money. Maybe moral limits are medicine.

### 3. Islam as Protection

Islamic limits should not be presented first as arbitrary prohibitions.

The better frame:

> Islam looks restrictive when you only ask, "What do I want right now?" It starts looking wise when you ask, "What does this protect?"

Examples:

- One glass may look harmless. A drinking culture does not.
- Gambling turns hope into a business model.
- Modesty is not shame. It is refusing to make the body the price of attention.
- Fasting makes hunger a teacher.
- Prayer is not escape from reality. It is a daily refusal to worship the world.
- In Islam, charity is not branding. It is obligation.

### 4. Fruit Test

The user identified Jesus's teaching to judge a tree by its fruits as a major unlock.

The method became:

> What did the seed produce when it became a civilization?

Important synthesis:

> Islamic civilization's rare fruit was high civilization with moral architecture built into the operating system.

### 5. Fruit Stack

The final major breakthrough was that the fruit is not a flat fact; the fruit is the stack.

Example:

> Muslims had hospitals.

Flat fact.

Fruit stack:

> care, free access in some cases, waqf funding, medical teaching, records, pharmacies, wards, libraries, service to the poor, and a moral vision that made healing a public duty.

Canonical line:

> The real fruit is the stack.

### 6. Lore Layer

The user named the importance of historical world-building: depth, texture, image, and emotional reality.

Canonical line:

> Lore is not decoration. Lore is the recovery of context, texture, and moral significance.

Example:

Retired working animals. A horse carried families, trade, food, travel, and survival. When it became old, a purely economic civilization could see uselessness. A mercy-based civilization sees service, gratitude, and a creature still owed care.

Line:

> Mercy after utility is one of civilization's highest tests.

### 7. Comparative Moral History

The user emphasized that Islam must not be studied in a vacuum. Comparative history is needed because people need benchmarks.

Canonical line:

> Context is not an excuse. Context is the measuring instrument.

Method:

- reconstruct the world Islam entered;
- identify what Islam corrected;
- compare fairly;
- avoid civilizational ego;
- preserve raw history in research;
- adapt public content by audience.

### 8. Raw Research, Wise Publication

Canonical line:

> Build with the full truth. Publish with audience wisdom.

Research layer should be raw, adult, and complete. Public layer should be calibrated.

## Source-Hardening Warnings

Do not publish the following without research:

- animal hospitals for cats/storks as a definitive broad claim;
- retired working animal institutions as widespread or unprecedented;
- all pre-Islamic Arabs commonly killed daughters;
- Muslims invented hospitals;
- Islam had one uninterrupted 1,400-year golden age;
- Islam uniquely invented animal welfare;
- non-Muslims were always treated equally under Muslim rule.

Use safe language:

- repeated golden ages, not one uninterrupted golden age;
- helped develop sophisticated hospitals, not invented hospitals;
- striking evidence of animal mercy, not unique invention of animal welfare;
- the Qur'an confronts child-killing and daughter disposability, while historians debate prevalence;
- premodern Muslim rule was not modern equality, but survival complicates total-erasure claims.
