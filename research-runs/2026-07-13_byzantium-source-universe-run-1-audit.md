# Deep Research Run Audit — Byzantium Source Universe, Run 1

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Status:** Reconnaissance draft accepted; source-first gate failed  
**Program:** Prophetic Civilizational Science  
**Related issues:** #50, #51

---

## 1. Executive verdict

The first Deep Research run generated a useful **lead map**, not a verified source universe.

It surfaced many plausible source clusters, archives, editions, comparison cases, and close-reading candidates. However, it did not satisfy the binding Source-First Deep Research Protocol because too many claims were supported through tertiary discovery pages, especially Wikipedia, rather than directly inspected primary sources, critical editions, official catalogs, or specialist scholarship.

The run must therefore be preserved as:

> **Discovery intelligence requiring source replacement and verification.**

It must not be treated as evidence, canon, or a completed Byzantine research packet.

---

## 2. What the run did well

### 2.1 It identified a plausible Byzantine core corpus

The report correctly oriented future work toward source clusters such as:

- the *Corpus Juris Civilis* and later Byzantine legal adaptations;
- Procopius across contrasting genres;
- *Theophanes* and continuation traditions;
- the *Basilika*;
- the *Book of the Eparch*;
- *De Ceremoniis*;
- Anna Komnene’s *Alexiad*;
- Niketas Choniates;
- the Pantokrator typikon;
- monastic foundation documents;
- coins, seals, inscriptions, and manuscript art;
- later Palaiologan historians and theological corpora.

This is a strong acquisition lead list.

### 2.2 It recognized genre conflict as evidence

The recommendation to read Procopius’s *Wars*, *Buildings*, and *Secret History* together is methodologically important. One author presenting imperial history, panegyric, and invective offers a built-in test against selecting the genre that best fits a preferred thesis.

### 2.3 It elevated the Pantokrator typikon

The report correctly recognized the Pantokrator typikon as a high-value pilot because it potentially connects:

- Christian moral motivation;
- endowment and funding;
- hospital design;
- staffing;
- wages or provisions;
- patient care;
- monastic and imperial administration.

It is a more falsifiable object than a broad claim such as “Christianity created charity.”

### 2.4 It retained the control-case logic

The report preserved the importance of Song China and several Islamic institutional ecologies as future controls against the inference that sophisticated administration, medicine, law, or commerce automatically certifies one theology.

### 2.5 It proposed useful repository objects

The suggested source-record fields, translation cards, claim atoms, dependency links, counterevidence links, and access-status flags align with Issue #51 and should inform implementation.

## 3. Why the run failed the source-first gate

### 3.1 Discovery sources were mistaken for evidentiary sources

A large share of the report’s specific claims and witness details were attached to Wikipedia pages or search-result summaries.

This violates the hierarchy:

- tertiary pages may identify a source;
- they may not verify manuscript history, edition authority, institutional details, or historical causation.

Every such claim must be marked **unverified lead** until replaced by the archive catalog, edition introduction, manuscript description, or specialist study.

### 3.2 Direct inspection status was not reliably recorded

The report did not consistently distinguish:

- source directly opened and read;
- official metadata inspected;
- search snippet only;
- secondary work mentioning the source;
- source named from general knowledge;
- source inaccessible or paywalled.

Without this distinction, a long bibliography creates false confidence.

### 3.3 “Original-language review” was promised but not performed

The report often marked sources as requiring Greek, Arabic, Persian, or Classical Chinese review. It did not actually provide:

- original passage;
- edition and passage locator;
- transliteration;
- literal gloss;
- translation variants;
- grammar notes;
- textual variants;
- interpretive options.

Therefore no translation-sensitive claim was source-closed.

### 3.4 Witness-chain claims outran provenance

Several claims about principal manuscripts, manuscript families, interpolations, or earliest surviving witnesses were presented without direct catalog or edition support.

These should be moved to a provisional field:

`reported_witness_claim — verification required`

No manuscript genealogy should enter canon until traced to an authoritative catalog, critical-edition preface, or specialist codicological study.

### 3.5 The run exceeded scope too early

The primary task was to establish the Byzantine source universe. The report expanded substantially into:

- Abbasid and Fatimid sources;
- Persianate endowments;
- Timbuktu chronicles;
- Song administrative corpora;
- full calibration-triangle design.

Those are valuable future branches, but the expansion reduced depth where the protocol demanded verification.

The first run should have preferred:

> five verified Byzantine source clusters over fifty lightly sourced comparative leads.

### 3.6 Critical editions were insufficiently specified

A true registry needs complete bibliographic identity:

- editor;
- title;
- series;
- volume;
- publisher;
- year;
- revision or edition number;
- passage system;
- manuscript basis;
- available translation and its source text.

Many entries contained abbreviated names such as “de Boor,” “Reiske,” or “Heimbach” without enough information for reproducible acquisition.

### 3.7 Source independence was discussed but not demonstrated

The report included a general dependency graph, but it did not perform a real witness audit for a load-bearing claim.

For example, a hospital claim should identify:

- the foundation document;
- independent contemporary testimony;
- later narrative dependence;
- architectural evidence;
- modern studies that rely on the same primary text;
- what remains known from only one witness.

### 3.8 Confidence arithmetic was invented prematurely

The proposed numeric penalties and multipliers were not empirically validated and risk giving subjective judgments a false scientific appearance.

Retain qualitative confidence and explicit reasons first. Numerical scoring should be introduced only after repeated calibration against expert-reviewed cases.

### 3.9 The research plan and completion state conflicted

The run was marked completed while stages involving critical editions, institutional records, and adversarial packets remained unfinished or only partially represented.

The final report should therefore be classified as:

> **Run 1A — broad reconnaissance, incomplete against planned gates.**

### 3.10 No primary-source excerpt was actually closed

The run did not finish one end-to-end chain of:

`claim -> passage -> original text -> edition -> witness -> translation -> context -> counterevidence -> confidence`

Until one such chain is completed, the research system has not yet proven itself.

## 4. Claims that remain unsafe

The report does not justify publishing any of the following as settled:

- Byzantium invented the hospital.
- The Byzantine hospital was equivalent to a modern hospital.
- Orthodox theology caused Byzantine longevity.
- Christianization materially humanized Roman law in a net sense.
- Byzantine gold coinage proves exceptional moral or theological alignment.
- the Pantokrator institution operated exactly as its typikon prescribed.
- Byzantine ritual cohesion produced political resilience.
- Song China or Islamic control cases have already been fairly matched to Byzantine periods.
- any manuscript listed in the report is necessarily the principal or earliest witness without catalog verification.

## 6. Correct next move — source-close one object

The next research unit should be deliberately small:

# **Pantokrator Typikon Source-Closure Pilot**

Why this object:

- it is concrete;
- it connects moral vision to funded institutional design;
- it permits Greek/translation comparison;
- it is central to the Byzantine hospital thesis;
- it can be checked against material remains and later testimony;
- it can reveal the difference between prescribed institution and lived practice;
- it will test the full repository pipeline before scaling.

Required end-to-end chain:

1. Identify the source’s exact title, date, issuing authority, and genre.
2. Locate the authoritative Greek edition.
3. Identify manuscript basis and known variants.
4. Locate the best scholarly English translation and identify its source edition.
5. Extract the hospital-related sections with exact passage locators.
6. Preserve original Greek, transliteration when useful, literal gloss, and published translation.
7. Atomize claims about wards, staffing, remuneration, patient categories, pharmacy, teaching, diet, and governance.
8. Separate prescription from evidence of implementation.
9. Find independent corroboration or contradiction.
10. Build a source-dependency graph for modern hospital claims.
11. Produce a thesis packet and breaker packet.
12. Record what remains unknowable.

## 7. Revised research sequence

### Run 1B — Pantokrator source closure

One source cluster; direct access and provenance required.

### Run 1C — Procopius adversarial genre packet

Close-read matched claims across *Wars*, *Buildings*, and *Secret History*.

### Run 1D — Justinianic law and Christian moral language

Select a limited legal topic rather than treating the entire corpus as one object.

Candidate topics:

- poor relief;
- slavery or manumission;
- marriage and family;
- religious coercion;
- treatment of vulnerable persons.

### Run 1E — Material controls

Coins, seals, inscriptions, institutional archaeology, and economic evidence.

### Run 2 — Byzantine period synthesis

Only after multiple source-closed packets exist.

### Run 3 — Calibration triangle

Only after matched Islamic and Song source packets are independently closed.

## 8. Standard for the next run

The next run passes only if it produces at least one reconstructable claim chain containing:

- exact primary passage;
- original language;
- critical edition;
- manuscript or edition basis;
- translation comparison;
- source genre and intent;
- independent corroboration status;
- opposing evidence;
- confidence rationale;
- safe public wording.

No amount of breadth may compensate for failure to complete this chain.

---

## 9. Canonical lesson

> **Deep Research succeeded at cartography and failed at excavation. The answer is not to abandon it, but to reduce the unit of work until provenance can defeat fluency.**

> **The first successful research run will not be the one that names the most sources. It will be the one that makes one important claim impossible to misrepresent.**
