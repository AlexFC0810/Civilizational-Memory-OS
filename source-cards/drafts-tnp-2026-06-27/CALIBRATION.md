# ADVERSARIAL CALIBRATION — 6 CLAIM-CARDS

Verdict scale: grade **HONEST** / **TOO GENEROUS** (downgrade) / **TOO HARSH** (could upgrade). Loyal to truth, not to the framing.

### 1. `quran_5_32_sanctity_of_life` — split A/B
**Grade HONEST.** The split (A for verbatim text + the documented Mishnah Sanhedrin 4:5 parallel; B for "shared ethic in practice") is exactly right. The overclaim_check correctly turns the Jewish-origin admission into a *feature* (kills the uniqueness trap) and mandates 5:33 travel with 5:32.
- **Residual issue (sharpen, don't downgrade):** the card asserts the Mishnah link as "mainstream (Witztum 2011, Reynolds 2018)" but the *direction* of borrowing is unsettled. The caveat says this; make sure the card BODY uses "intertextual engagement / shared late-antique milieu," never "echoes" phrased as chronological dependence — "openly echoing the Jewish teaching" in the one_line slightly implies Quran-knew-Mishnah. Soften to "paralleling / converging with." One-word fix, not a regrade.
- **Whitewash check:** none. This is the cleanest card.

### 2. `manumission_paths_in_islam` — B+
**Grade slightly TOO GENEROUS at the headline; the card's own internals are honest, so the fix is a half-step, not a gutting.** The A-grade primary anchors are real (24:33, 9:60, 90:13, expiation verses all verifiable). But "B+" advertises more confidence than the load-bearing truth supports, because the *interesting* claim ("unusually many incentives… built-in engine") is the contested Jonathan Brown thesis, and the verb in the title — "incentivized" — is fine, but "engine that incentivized freeing slaves" flirts with the teleology Kecia Ali demolishes (no internally-developed abolition critique).
- **Recommend: down to B.** Rationale: the *mechanisms* are A; the *narrative weight* ("turbines"/"engine") is C-contested; a card's grade should track the claim it will actually be deployed to make, and this one will be deployed as "Islam had an emancipation engine." The card disarms this in caveats, but the grade should reflect the deployable claim, not the safest sub-claim.
- **Mandatory caveat already present and good:** persistence dates (1962/1981) + Manning counter-voice. **Add the Kecia Ali line explicitly** (from links review #6): "no strong internally-developed religious critique of slaveholding drove abolition." That is THE concession that converts this from apologetics to rigor, and it's currently only implied.

### 3. `mamluks_slave_to_sultan` — B
**Grade HONEST, possibly even slightly TOO HARSH but keep at B for safety.** This is well-anchored institutional history (Ayalon/Amitai/Stilt), the caveats are unusually disciplined ("still slavery," "military caste not general mobility," awlad al-nas barred → trade kept running), and it pre-deflates the Delhi "Slave Dynasty" label. - **Residual issue:** the card is institutional history, NOT a revelation/hadith anchor, and the repo grades pure historiography conservatively (cf. the ijaza card capped at B for the same reason). B is correct and consistent. **Do not upgrade.**
- **Whitewash check:** actively anti-whitewash — this is the model card for "mobility ≠ liberation." No change.

### 4. `spread_of_islam_indonesia_trade_sufi` — B+
**Grade HONEST.** "Conquest is not conversion, NOT 'Islam never spread by force'" is the correct narrow claim, and building Aceh/Demak/Mataram warfare + Ibn Battuta's c.1346 jihad note INTO the claim (not as footnotes) is exactly right. Keep B+ only if the card body states the weighting is contested *in the claim line itself*, not just the caveat. Otherwise B. Borderline; I'd hold B+ since the honesty anchors are load-bearing in-claim.
- **Whitewash check:** none. The scale-discipline guardrail ("refutes 'only,' not 'never'") is excellent.

### 5. `slavery_islamic_vs_chattel_comparison` — C
**Grade HONEST — this is the single most important grade in the set and it is correctly held low.** C is the right call precisely because this is the most abuse-prone topic and the "different architecture → milder crime" slide is the exact apologetic reflex. The card hard-blocks it ("a different architecture is not a gentler crime"), forces statistics into ranges, keeps Lewis's documentation of real anti-black prejudice in, and flags Brown/Ali as contested-cite-to-show-debate-exists-never-to-resolve.
- **Residual risk (the one genuine vulnerability):** the comparison FORMAT itself is the danger — any "Islamic vs. Atlantic" framing structurally invites the audience to hear "less bad," no matter how many caveats ride along. The card knows this. **Recommendation: keep C AND add a deployment gate** — this card is reference/internal-canon and debate-defense ONLY; it must NOT be the spine of a standalone viral clip. The comparison should only ever appear *responsively* (when an interlocutor raises it), never *proactively*. That's a deployment-readiness restriction, not a regrade.
- **Whitewash check:** the card is the most caveated; the residual concealment risk is concubinage-consent — correctly named as "the single most-whitewashed point." Hold.

### 6. `quran_vs_hadith_authority` — B+
**Grade HONEST.** The grade-split (A for the descriptive claim that Sunni usul + secular text-criticism both rank Quran above hadith in transmission-certainty; B/C for "preservation" as divine guarantee) is rigorous. Naming the Sana'a palimpsest's pre-Uthmanic variants and foregrounding Motzki (hadith credibly early, higher Quran certainty ≠ any hadith false) blocks the smear-inversion.

- **Whitewash check:** none, and it's symmetric (carves out Quranist/Shia, doesn't smear hadith). This is a meta/method card, not a substance claim — lowest abuse surface of the set.
