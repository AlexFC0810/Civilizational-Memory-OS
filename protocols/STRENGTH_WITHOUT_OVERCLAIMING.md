# Strength Without Overclaiming

**Status:** Canonical communication protocol
**Binding (2026-07-11):** adopted as the human taste layer **bound under** the deployment gate. The overclaiming failure mode is mechanically scanned by `scripts/evals.mjs`; the underclaiming failure mode this document names is caught by human/frontier review during grading. Neither mode is fixed by weakening the gate.

## Why this exists

Truth discipline must never become rhetorical self-disarmament.

The repository has two failure modes:

1. **Overclaiming** — presenting uncertain, local, or weakly sourced material as universal fact.
2. **Underclaiming** — withholding strong, historically meaningful claims until every archival question is closed.

Both weaken truth.

> The operating standard is not maximal caution. It is maximum justified force.

## The key correction

“Not fully source-closed” does **not** mean “cannot be mentioned.” It means the wording must match the evidence type.

The system should distinguish:

- what is established;
- what is strongly evidenced;
- what is tradition-level evidence;
- what is a documented example but not a civilization-wide norm;
- what is a live hypothesis;
- what is folklore or promotional repetition.

The goal is to say the strongest true thing now, while keeping the path open to strengthen or revise it later.

## The Evidence-to-Expression Ladder

### Tier 1 — Directly established

Use declarative language.

Examples:

- “The Qur'an describes animals and birds as communities.”
- “Major Sunni hadith collections preserve stories condemning animal starvation and rewarding care for a thirsty dog.”
- “Islamic jurists discussed duties concerning feeding, loading, slaughter, hunting, and animal ownership.”

### Tier 2 — Strongly documented historical pattern

Use confident synthesis with bounded scope.

Examples:

- “Premodern Islamic civilization developed a dense animal-mercy tradition spanning scripture, hadith, law, market regulation, literature, veterinary knowledge, and charitable practice.”
- “Ottoman architecture incorporated purpose-built structures for birds across numerous religious and civic buildings.”
- “Hisba literature treated market and transport practices, including animal treatment, as matters of public regulation.”

### Tier 3 — Documented case, uncertain prevalence

Name the case strongly; do not universalize it.

Examples:

- “Bursa preserved the memory and institution of a hospice for injured storks.”
- “Some endowments and urban practices supported the feeding or care of animals.”
- “Particular Islamic cities and patrons created animal-care institutions.”

Avoid replacing a real case with weak language merely because its empire-wide prevalence is unknown.

### Tier 4 — Tradition or reception fact

State what the civilization taught, remembered, or canonized even when event historicity is debated.

Examples:

- “Muslim moral memory canonized the image of an exhausted camel whose suffering could indict its owner.”
- “The tradition taught generations that mercy to a dog could outweigh social disgrace.”

This is a real historical fact about moral formation even when the exact seventh-century event cannot be independently verified.

### Tier 5 — Plausible research frontier

Use hypothesis language without burying the significance.

Examples:

- “There is evidence worth testing that some waqfs maintained animals after productive use.”
- “Retirement sanctuaries may represent one of the strongest premodern expressions of a post-use obligation.”

### Tier 6 — Unsupported or promotional

Do not deploy as fact.

Examples:

- “The Ottomans created the world's first free animal hospital,” unless the comparative and institutional claim is source-closed.
- “Every retired animal received sanctuary.”

## Four kinds of truth in transmitted stories

A hadith or historical story can carry several independent truths:

1. **Event truth** — did the narrated event occur as described?
2. **Corpus truth** — is the report securely present in a known collection?
3. **Reception truth** — was it widely taught, cited, preached, or used in law and ethics?
4. **Normative truth** — what moral principle did the community encode through it?

Do not let uncertainty in event truth erase reception or normative truth.

> A story can be historically uncertain as eyewitness reportage and historically certain as a civilization-shaping moral memory.

## Comparative priority

The primary benchmark is contemporaneous comparison.

Compare Islamic periods with:

- Byzantine and late Roman worlds;
- medieval Latin Christendom;
- Jewish communities and law;
- Persian and Central Asian societies;
- Jain, Hindu, and Buddhist India;
- Chinese and steppe societies.

Modern comparison is a second layer used to identify progress, regression, industrial scale, and unrealized potential.

Do not use modern law as the default baseline for judging whether a premodern achievement was exceptional.

## The Force Calibration Test

Before publishing a sentence, ask:

1. What is the strongest evidence class supporting it?
2. What is the widest scope actually demonstrated?
3. Does one qualifier preserve truth without draining force?
4. Are we withholding a real achievement merely because it is not universal?
5. Would we demand the same level of proof before repeating a hostile claim?
6. Is the sentence memorable enough to compete with propaganda?

## Strong default thesis for Islamic animal ethics

> Premodern Islamic civilization developed one of history's richest integrated traditions of animal mercy: animals were imagined as communities and worshippers, cruelty could threaten salvation, owners were assigned affirmative duties, animal suffering entered law and moral storytelling, and some cities translated compassion into architecture, regulation, charity, and care institutions.

This thesis does not claim universal compliance or exclusive invention. It is strong because the integrated constellation is itself historically significant.

## Anti-bureaucracy rule

Research gates exist to prevent falsehood, not to delay every meaningful sentence.

- Publish Tier 1–2 claims now.
- Publish Tier 3–4 claims with attribution and scope.
- Investigate Tier 5 claims aggressively.
- Reject Tier 6 claims until evidence changes.

## Canonical close

> We will not trade truth for triumphalism. We will also not trade power for timidity. Say the strongest true thing the evidence permits—and make it memorable enough to defeat the lie.
