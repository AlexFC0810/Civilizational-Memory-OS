# Thought Corpus to Epistemic Twin Protocol

**Status:** Working protocol  
**Purpose:** Convert a creator's lifetime public or consented private corpus into a source-grounded, versioned, corrigible intellectual model without impersonation, flattening, or false certainty.

## 1. Intake and consent class

Classify the project before ingestion:

- **Public archival model:** public corpus only; no claim of creator approval.
- **Participating creator model:** creator reviews, corrects, and supplies context.
- **Private creator OS:** includes nonpublic material under explicit access rules.
- **Historical/deceased thinker model:** public or licensed corpus; no synthetic endorsement.

Record:

- corpus rights and access;
- permitted outputs;
- identity and voice restrictions;
- public/private boundaries;
- correction contact;
- whether first-person generation is prohibited or explicitly approved.

Default: no public synthetic first-person speech.

## 2. Build the source manifest

For every item record:

- source ID;
- title;
- date;
- URL or file reference;
- format;
- host and participants;
- transcript status;
- rights/reuse status;
- topic tags;
- reliability notes;
- whether edited, clipped, or complete.

Prioritize full-length and chronological sources over viral clips.

## 3. Normalize the corpus

- transcribe;
- identify speakers;
- preserve timestamps;
- flag uncertain transcription;
- restore references where possible;
- separate quote, paraphrase, sarcasm, storytelling, and hypothetical speech;
- link clips back to full context;
- deduplicate reposts and repeated material.

Do not allow an ASR error to become a belief.

## 4. Extract intellectual units

Atomize:

- factual claims;
- causal claims;
- moral judgments;
- definitions;
- historical interpretations;
- predictions;
- comparisons;
- analogies;
- values;
- source endorsements;
- source rejections;
- unresolved questions;
- concessions;
- corrections;
- self-descriptions;
- calls to action.

Each unit receives a stable ID and provenance.

## 5. Build the claim-family graph

Merge recurring variants into durable claim families while preserving date and wording.

For each family record:

- strongest formulation;
- weakest or most rhetorical variant;
- recurrence;
- supporting sources;
- explicit confidence;
- implied premises;
- connected claims;
- audience contexts;
- later modifications;
- current verification state.

## 6. Build the mind map

Map the person's:

- foundational assumptions;
- core values;
- moral priorities;
- recurring questions;
- major causal models;
- historical narrative;
- comparison classes;
- attention allocation;
- trusted institutions and source types;
- suspected capture mechanisms;
- characteristic metaphors;
- strongest insights;
- recurring blind spots;
- unresolved frontiers.

The goal is not personality typing. It is intellectual architecture.

## 7. Preserve temporal evolution

Never produce one timeless persona from a changing corpus.

For every major position identify:

- first known expression;
- major restatements;
- contradiction or transition;
- current expression;
- stated reason for change;
- inferred reason, clearly labeled;
- remaining uncertainty.

Create versioned snapshots where change is material.

## 8. Source and truth audit

For priority claims:

- identify the authority layer;
- verify quotations and statistics;
- separate primary from secondary sources;
- identify true fragments and unsupported bridges;
- test alternative causal explanations;
- include the strongest opponent case;
- identify breaker evidence;
- grade confidence;
- record prohibited overclaims.

Do not turn fidelity to the creator into loyalty to the creator's errors.

## 9. Contradiction and tension audit

Classify apparent contradictions:

- genuine contradiction;
- change over time;
- different scope;
- different audience or rhetorical register;
- unresolved ambiguity;
- transcript/context error;
- complementary positions mistakenly treated as conflict.

Present unresolved tensions to the creator where possible.

## 10. Generate the six modes

Every answer must expose its mode:

- Archive;
- Reconstruction;
- Audit;
- Augmentation;
- Creator-Approved;
- Council.

Never silently cross modes.

Required label examples:

- **Documented position:** directly supported by corpus.
- **Best reconstruction:** strongly implied across sources.
- **Tentative inference:** plausible but not confirmed.
- **AIOS augmentation:** new synthesis not attributable to creator.
- **Creator-approved update:** explicitly affirmed after review.

## 11. Creator validation loop

For participating creators, ask them to review:

- top claims;
- core values;
- causal map;
- intellectual timeline;
- contradictions;
- strongest synthesis;
- blind spots;
- public/private boundaries;
- preferred correction language.

Record disagreements between creator self-understanding and corpus evidence rather than overwriting either.

## 12. Build the augmentation layer

Generate:

- missing evidence;
- neglected counterexamples;
- cross-disciplinary connections;
- historical parallels;
- stronger formulations;
- new load-bearing questions;
- collaboration opportunities;
- content opportunities;
- institutional implications;
- research backlog.

The augmentation layer must remain separable from the person's own view.

## 13. Council-of-Minds synthesis

When comparing multiple minds:

1. normalize definitions;
2. match claim families;
3. identify convergence;
4. isolate unique truth fragments;
5. separate orthogonal from contradictory explanations;
6. reveal hidden premises;
7. compare evidence quality;
8. identify breaker evidence;
9. create an integrated model;
10. preserve dissent where unresolved.

Do not average conclusions or reward repetition.

## 14. Deployment products

Possible outputs:

- searchable creator archive;
- belief and causal map;
- evolution timeline;
- strongest-synthesis brief;
- contradiction ledger;
- source repair queue;
- episode prep;
- debate prep;
- book or documentary research room;
- audience FAQ;
- public claim cards;
- clip and story library;
- guest and podcast opportunity map;
- Creator Supercharge Packet;
- Council-of-Minds report.

## 15. Evaluation

### Fidelity

- Can the creator recognize the model as fair?
- Can independent readers trace claims to sources?
- Does the model preserve change over time?
- Does it distinguish explicit belief from inference?

### Truth improvement

- How many errors were caught?
- Which claims gained stronger evidence?
- Which claims were narrowed, corrected, or retired?
- Did the model surface decisive counterevidence?

### Insight

- Did the system recover forgotten connections?
- Did Augmentation Mode generate genuinely new questions?
- Did Council Mode produce a synthesis unavailable from one corpus?

### Utility

- preparation time saved;
- creator adoption;
- asset reuse;
- audience learning;
- correction speed;
- opportunities created;
- institutional actions generated.

## 16. Failure modes

- persona cosplay;
- fake quotations;
- present-day views projected backward;
- smoothing away contradiction;
- loyalty capture;
- hallucinated private beliefs;
- confusing rhetorical style with substantive belief;
- ingesting clips without full context;
- treating frequency as truth;
- averaging disagreements;
- hidden Augmentation Mode;
- building software before manual fidelity is proven.

## 17. Minimum viable pilot

For one creator:

- 20–50 high-value long-form sources;
- source manifest;
- normalized transcript corpus;
- 100 priority claims;
- 20 recurring questions;
- 10 causal models;
- 10 strongest unique insights;
- 10 contradictions or evolution points;
- 10 source-repair needs;
- one strongest-synthesis brief;
- one live Creator Supercharge Packet;
- one creator validation session;
- one Council comparison.

## Canonical standard

> **Fidelity without audit creates a smarter echo chamber. Audit without fidelity destroys the human insight we came to preserve. The Epistemic Twin must do both.**
