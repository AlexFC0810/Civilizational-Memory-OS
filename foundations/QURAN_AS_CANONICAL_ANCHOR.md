## 1. The clarification

When this project says that the Qur'an is the **single source of truth**, the intended claim is not:

> “No truth can be learned from history, reason, observation, scholarship, or any other text.”

The intended claim is:

> **For this Qur'an-first project, the Qur'an is the sole bounded canonical corpus treated as direct divine speech and the final audit standard for claims attributed to God. Every other source is humanly transmitted, interpreted, reconstructed, or observed and must be labeled accordingly.**

This is a claim about **divine authority and source identity**, not a claim that the Qur'an is an exhaustive encyclopedia of every historical, scientific, legal, or institutional question.

---

## 2. Canonical simplicity

A central power of the Qur'anic architecture is **canonical simplicity**:

- one bounded scripture;
- a stable shared recitational object across the global community;
- public memorization and repeated communal recitation;
- no priestly class with exclusive custody of the text;
- a common return point when inherited authorities conflict.

> **The simpler the canonical root, the easier it is for ordinary people to audit what is being attributed to God.**

Complex traditions can preserve enormous wisdom. They also increase the risk of:

- source confusion;
- authority laundering;
- contradictory reports;
- political capture;
- interpretive dependency;
- inherited opinion being presented as revelation;
- ordinary believers losing the ability to inspect the root claim.

The objective is not to abolish complexity. It is to prevent complexity from silently acquiring divine status.

---

## 3. Canonical checksum

The Qur'an functions as a **canonical checksum**.

Whenever a person says:

- “God commands this”;
- “Islam requires this”;
- “This is the unquestionable divine rule”;
- “A Muslim must believe this historical detail”;

run the claim through five checks:

1. **Presence:** Is it actually in the Qur'an?
2. **Attribution:** Is the statement direct, inferred, or imported from another source?
3. **Context:** What passage, audience, and problem surround it?
4. **Coherence:** Does the interpretation fit the Qur'an's wider moral and theological architecture?
5. **Status:** Is a human report, ruling, custom, or political interest being elevated into God's own speech?

> **No claim may enter the divine layer without Qur'anic warrant.**

## 4. Simple authority, plural evidence

The architecture can be expressed as:

# **Simple authority; plural evidence.**

### Simple authority

The Qur'an is the single canonical divine anchor used to adjudicate what this project attributes directly to God.

### Plural evidence

Other questions require other evidence:

- history for what probably happened;
- language for what words meant;
- manuscripts for textual transmission;
- hadith and sira for transmitted Muslim memory;
- tafsir and fiqh for interpretive history;
- institutions and data for actual outcomes;
- reason and moral analysis for coherence and application.

These sources can inform, illuminate, challenge, or reconstruct. They cannot be mislabeled as Qur'anic speech.

## 5. Previous revelation and the present Bible

The Qur'an affirms that God revealed the Torah and gave Jesus the Gospel, describing them as containing guidance and light. It also presents the Qur'an as confirming previous scripture and as `muhaymin`—a guardian, criterion, or supreme authority over it. See Qur'an 5:44, 5:46, and 5:48.

The Qur'an also accuses some people of:

- knowingly distorting words after understanding them (2:75);
- writing material and attributing it to God (2:79);
- twisting the Book with their tongues so listeners think something belongs to scripture when it does not (3:78);
- concealing or displacing words in other passages.

The safest Qur'an-first formulation is therefore:

> **The original revelations given to earlier prophets were from God. The present Biblical canons are historically transmitted collections containing ancient religious texts, but they are not treated by this project as an independently sufficient or uniformly preserved divine checksum. The Qur'an remains the controlling criterion.**

### Integrity boundary

Do not compress this into:

> “The Bible was edited many times, so everything in it is false.”

That claim is neither required by the Qur'an nor historically defensible.

Muslim interpreters have differed over whether Qur'anic accusations of `tahrif` refer principally to:

- textual alteration;
- distortion of meaning;
- selective concealment;
- false oral attribution;
- or combinations of these.

The Hebrew Bible and New Testament have complex manuscript histories and identifiable textual variants. Textual criticism can recover a great deal, but the current Bible is not one single book delivered verbatim to Jesus or Moses in its present canonical form.

The project should distinguish:

1. the original Torah and Gospel as Qur'anically affirmed revelations;
2. the surviving Biblical manuscript traditions;
3. later canon formation, translation, commentary, and doctrine;
4. what the Qur'an confirms, corrects, rejects, or leaves unaddressed.

## 6. Preservation claim: doctrinal and historical layers

The Qur'an states that God revealed the Reminder and will preserve it (15:9). Muslims understand the Qur'an's preservation through intertwined oral and written transmission.

Historical research strongly supports an early standardized Qur'anic consonantal text and the existence of very early manuscripts substantially aligned with the received text.

However, a truth-first project should not turn preservation into the brittle claim:

> “There has never been any variant reading, orthographic difference, manuscript variant, or history of canonization.”

Canonical `qira'at`, early orthographic variation, and manuscript evidence are real areas of study.

The stronger formulation is:

> **The Qur'an possesses an unusually early, public, and highly stable written-recited transmission, while its exact textual history—including canonical readings and early variants—should be described accurately rather than erased.**

Theological certainty and historical description must be labeled rather than collapsed.

---

## 7. The interpretation remainder

One book does not eliminate interpretation.

People still differ over:

- Arabic vocabulary and grammar;
- passage context;
- general versus specific language;
- legal versus moral application;
- apparent tensions;
- difficult verses;
- translation;
- whether a principle is timeless or context-specific.

Therefore canonical simplicity solves **source sprawl**, but not every form of disagreement.

> **The Qur'an is a common audit surface, not an excuse to pretend interpretation has disappeared.**

The project must expose the interpretive path from verse to conclusion.

---

## 8. Relationship to Sunni, Shia, and Qur'an-only positions

Mainstream Sunni and Shia traditions do not treat the Qur'an as the only authoritative religious source in practice. They assign major authority to Prophetic tradition, though they differ over transmission corpora, authorities, and legal methods.

Strict Qur'an-only approaches are minority positions.

This project must therefore say openly:

> **“Qur'an-first” is our declared epistemic and canonical operating stance; it should not be falsely presented as the uncontested method of every Muslim tradition.**

Traditions should be represented fairly, even when the project does not grant every source equal divine authority.

---

## 9. Complexity budget

Every additional source layer spends from a **complexity budget**.

A layer earns inclusion when it provides enough value to justify its risks.

Score it on:

- necessity;
- source proximity;
- reliability;
- independence;
- explanatory value;
- auditability;
- distortion risk;
- accessibility to ordinary people.

This produces a disciplined rule:

> **Add complexity only when it answers a question the canonical root does not answer by itself—and label the added layer every time.**

## 10. Public language

### 15-second version

> The Qur'an is our single canonical source of divine speech. History, hadith, scholarship, and evidence can inform us, but they must never be smuggled into “God says” without Qur'anic warrant.

### 60-second version

> When we call the Qur'an the single source of truth, we mean the single bounded text we treat as direct divine revelation and the final audit point for claims attributed to God. That does not mean history or scholarship are useless. They answer different questions. Hadith can show what later Muslims transmitted; fiqh can show how jurists reasoned; history can show what civilizations built. But none of those sources should be silently quoted as though it were the Qur'an. The power is canonical simplicity: one shared return point, with every additional layer clearly labeled.

### Master line

> **The Qur'an anchors the truth claim. Every other source must show its name tag.**

## 12. Final doctrine

> **God is the ultimate source of truth. The Qur'an is this project's single canonical corpus of divine speech and its common audit layer. Other sources remain necessary for historical, linguistic, interpretive, and empirical questions, but they are humanly mediated and must be visibly labeled. Canonical simplicity protects the root; source transparency prevents the branches from impersonating it.**
