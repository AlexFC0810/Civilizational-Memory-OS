## 1. Brand decision

### Recommended public brand

# **Islam, Clearly**

### Primary tagline

> **Qur'an first. History whole. Claims made precise.**

### Supporting line

> A truth-first guide to what Islam teaches, what Muslims built, where they failed, and what should be restored.

### Why this name

The brand is:

- immediately understandable to a cold audience;
- broad enough for theology, history, propaganda immunity, and restoration;
- non-combative;
- compatible with Muslims, Christians, skeptics, students, and journalists;
- distinct from a debate channel or apologetics brand;
- naturally aligned with clarity, source separation, and first principles.

It does not promise that every question is simple. It promises that every claim will be made more precise.

## 4. Positioning

Islam, Clearly is not:

- a fatwa service;
- a sectarian authority;
- an automated preacher;
- a debate-win archive;
- a repository of flattering trivia;
- a denial engine for Muslim failures;
- a hostile catalogue of Muslim failures.

It is:

> **A public reasoning system for separating revelation, history, interpretation, institutions, conduct, and outcomes.**

Its differentiator is not merely better answers.

It exposes:

- how the question was decomposed;
- which source layer each claim belongs to;
- the strongest evidence on both sides;
- the confidence boundary;
- what would change the verdict;
- what historical capability should be restored.

## 9. Visual identity

### Mood

- Golden-Age scholar aesthetic without nostalgia cosplay;
- manuscript precision combined with modern interface clarity;
- warm, luminous, calm, serious, human;
- no militant, royal, or triumphalist imagery;
- no generic crescent-and-mosque identity as the entire visual language.

### Visual motifs

- illuminated margins;
- geometric indexing systems;
- constellations and transmission networks;
- roots, branches, and fruit;
- maps of knowledge movement;
- manuscript annotations;
- architectural light and courtyards;
- water as guidance, renewal, and public good.

### Typography

Use a highly readable contemporary text face paired with a restrained scholarly serif. Arabic typography should be treated as primary design, not ornament.

### Brand voice

- morally serious;
- epistemically humble;
- memorable but not sensational;
- candid about uncertainty;
- protective of victims;
- equally resistant to smear and apologetics;
- accessible to a cold audience.

### Phase 1 — Claims Lab

Add structured claim cards with search, filters, confidence labels, and version history.

### Phase 2 — Civilization and restoration

Add institutional pattern pages and restoration proposals.

### Phase 3 — interactive inquiry

Allow users to submit a claim and receive:

- source classification;
- related hardened cards;
- unanswered research questions;
- a warning when no verdict is source-closed.

Do not generate authoritative religious rulings or public accusations autonomously.

## 11. Success metrics

Do not optimize solely for traffic.

Measure whether users can:

- distinguish Qur'an from hadith, law, state, and conduct;
- reject both a smear and an apologetic overclaim;
- recall a load-bearing question;
- identify confidence and uncertainty;
- transfer the method to a new claim;
- name one restoration target;
- trust corrections rather than interpret them as weakness.

---

## 12. North star

> **Make Islam understandable without making it simplistic; defensible without making it dishonest; historically rich without making it nostalgic; and spiritually rooted without making human interpretation unaccountable.**
