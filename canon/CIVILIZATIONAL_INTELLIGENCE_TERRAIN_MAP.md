# Civilizational Intelligence Terrain Map

**Status:** Canonical map  
**Purpose:** Define the full terrain before assigning deep research to Codex, Claude Code, or human contributors.

> The repository should not behave like a pile of essays. It should behave like a mapped civilization-scale research program with known domains, interfaces, evidence standards, and build order.

---

## 1. The map / terrain distinction

- **The map** is the ontology: what domains exist, how they connect, what questions belong where, and what outputs must be produced.
- **The terrain** is the actual evidence: primary sources, datasets, manuscripts, legal traditions, historical cases, causal analysis, and unresolved contradictions.
- **Scouts** gather evidence from a bounded region.
- **Cartographers** integrate it into the system.
- **Red teams** search for breaker evidence.
- **Editors** compress only what survives into public claims.

The immediate priority is map completeness, not exhaustive research in every region.

---

## 2. The seven continental domains

### A. Epistemic Authority

What counts as evidence, who may interpret it, and how confidence is assigned.

Subdomains:
- revelation and scripture;
- oral and written transmission;
- canon formation;
- hadith, sira, tafsir, fiqh, consensus, custom;
- manuscript evidence;
- historical probability vs normative authority;
- expert consensus and institutional capture;
- propaganda, framing, and information integrity.

Core question:
> How do we know what a civilization actually taught, believed, practiced, and transmitted?

### B. Moral Architecture

How a worldview defines the good and binds conduct under temptation.

Subdomains:
- dignity and moral status;
- justice, mercy, truth, courage, restraint;
- duties to poor, orphan, widow, stranger, prisoner, disabled, elderly, animal, enemy;
- sexuality, family, appetite, wealth, debt, and power;
- taqwa, conscience, shame, honor, virtue, rights;
- moral realism, divine command, natural law, humanism.

Core question:
> What binds a person when goodness is costly, evil is profitable, and nobody is watching?

### C. Power and Governance

How systems acquire, distribute, restrain, and correct power.

Subdomains:
- state formation;
- law and courts;
- democracy, monarchy, caliphate, party-state, anarchy;
- clerical power;
- military power and war ethics;
- oligarchy, money, media, surveillance, bureaucracy;
- legitimacy, dissent, rebellion, accountability;
- corruption and elite capture.

Core question:
> What prevents rulers, institutions, tribes, markets, and majorities from becoming morally absolute?

### D. Material Capacity

The economic and technological substrate of civilization.

Subdomains:
- food, energy, water, health, sanitation;
- agriculture, industry, trade, markets;
- property, labor, slavery, debt, taxation;
- welfare, zakat, waqf, charity, insurance;
- education and literacy;
- communications, transportation, weapons;
- abundance, scarcity, inequality, resilience.

Core question:
> What can a society materially sustain, and what moral choices become possible or impossible under its constraints?

### E. Social Reproduction and Meaning

How civilizations regenerate people, trust, identity, and purpose.

Subdomains:
- family formation;
- fertility and intergenerational continuity;
- ritual, worship, festivals, story, memory;
- community, congregation, guild, union, neighborhood;
- loneliness, atomization, addiction, nihilism;
- education, initiation, role models, sacred time;
- inherited moral capital and its regeneration.

Core question:
> Can the civilization reproduce not only bodies and skills, but trustworthy, meaning-bearing people?

### F. Conflict, Violence, and Peace Mechanisms

How violence is generated, displaced, restrained, hidden, or exported.

Subdomains:
- homicide, feud, war, genocide, repression;
- policing, prison, surveillance, censorship;
- welfare and desperation reduction;
- commerce and interdependence;
- conscience, shame, honor, deterrence;
- colonial and proxy violence;
- reporting bias and hidden coercion;
- self-domestication and gene–culture coevolution.

Core question:
> Peace by what mechanism, at what cost, and with what displaced harms?

### G. Learning, Error Correction, and Renewal

How civilizations detect failure and update without destroying themselves.

Subdomains:
- science and falsification;
- prophetic critique and reform;
- elections, markets, courts, free speech;
- repentance, renewal, revival, revolution;
- institutional feedback loops;
- propaganda as feedback corruption;
- historical memory;
- versioned truth closure.

Core question:
> How does the system discover that it is wrong, and what permits correction before collapse?

## 3. Cross-cutting lenses

Every domain must be evaluated through the same lenses:

1. **Blueprint** — claimed ideal or source architecture.
2. **Builder** — interpreters, leaders, classes, institutions.
3. **Building** — actual social system produced.
4. **Baseline** — what was normal in comparable societies and periods.
5. **Mechanism** — how the outcome was generated.
6. **Fruit** — visible and hidden consequences.
7. **Corruption mode** — predictable failure under power.
8. **Correction mode** — capacity to expose and repair failure.
9. **Trajectory** — what the system tends to reproduce over time.
10. **Breaker evidence** — what would overturn the current assessment.

---

## 4. The civilization benchmark

A civilization should be scored on at least seven dimensions:

- **Reality alignment** — ability to discover and accept truth.
- **Power restraint** — ability to prevent domination and capture.
- **Vulnerability protection** — treatment of beings with low leverage.
- **Material competence** — ability to sustain life, health, knowledge, and resilience.
- **Social reproduction** — ability to reproduce families, trust, community, and competence.
- **Meaning and costly morality** — ability to motivate sacrifice beyond private advantage.
- **Error correction** — ability to detect, admit, and repair failure.

No single variable—religiosity, secularism, GDP, democracy, literacy, or technology—may substitute for the full benchmark.

## 5. Priority research theaters

### Theater 1 — Islamic epistemic authority
Why first: almost every controversy depends on whether Qur’an, Sunnah, hadith, law, empire, and custom are distinguished correctly.

### Theater 2 — Slavery as a stock-and-flow system
Why first: it tests historical baseline, moral trajectory, material constraints, legal structure, and implementation failure simultaneously.

### Theater 3 — Violence-reduction mechanisms
Why first: it separates conscience, trust, welfare, abundance, surveillance, coercion, and exported violence.

### Theater 4 — Inherited moral capital
Why first: it tests whether secular success is generated independently, inherited, regenerated, or partially depleted.

### Theater 5 — Revelation and worldview compression
Why first: it transforms isolated apologetic arguments into a cumulative explanatory comparison.

### Theater 6 — Propaganda-immunity evals
Why first: it converts recurring smears and frame collapses into a reusable benchmark for humans and AI systems.

## 6. Build sequence

### Phase 0 — Map
- define domains;
- define claims and ontologies;
- identify interfaces;
- create research packet templates;
- create confidence and citation rules.

### Phase 1 — Scout
- assign bounded research packets;
- gather primary and high-quality secondary sources;
- capture counterevidence;
- preserve uncertainty.

### Phase 2 — Integrate
- build evidence graphs;
- reconcile contradictions;
- update claim cards;
- identify missing variables and confounders.

### Phase 3 — Benchmark
- score cases using common dimensions;
- test comparison symmetry;
- run breaker examples;
- publish provisional verdicts.

### Phase 4 — Deploy
- essays;
- visual atlases;
- debate packets;
- public claim cards;
- datasets and dashboards;
- AI evals and training artifacts.

### Phase 5 — Renew
- version every verdict;
- monitor new scholarship;
- preserve changelogs;
- retire brittle claims;
- reopen closed questions when breaker evidence appears.

---

## 7. Assignment rule for agents

Never ask an agent to “research Islam,” “research slavery,” or “research secularism.”

Assign a bounded cell:

> One exact question + one source hierarchy + one comparison class + one output schema + one falsifier requirement.

The correct unit of delegation is not a topic. It is a **research packet**.

---

## 8. Canonical synthesis

> Civilizational Memory OS is the map, evidence compiler, benchmark laboratory, and error-correction layer for studying how societies discover truth, restrain power, protect the vulnerable, generate material competence, reproduce meaning, reduce violence, and renew themselves across generations.

> Map first. Scout second. Integrate third. Publish only what survives.
