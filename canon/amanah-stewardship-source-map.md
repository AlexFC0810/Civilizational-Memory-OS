# Amanah & Stewardship Source Map

## Status
Research scaffold for source-hardening Amanah-Driven Building inside the History of Islamic Civilization OS.

This file is not the final argument. It is the source map and research protocol.

## Purpose
Build a historically grounded map of Islamic concepts related to trust, stewardship, accountability, purification, excellence, knowledge, commerce, animals, public good, and civilizational order.

The goal is to help people understand Islam and Islamic civilization through source-backed moral architecture, not stereotypes or shallow summaries.

## Primary source anchors

### Qur'an 33:72 — Amanah / the Trust
Research question: How have classical and modern interpreters understood the Trust placed on humanity?

### Qur'an 91:7-10 — the soul, corruption, purification
Research question: How does the Qur'anic language of the soul connect to tazkiyah, moral agency, and responsibility?

### Qur'an 51:56 — worship/service as purpose frame
Research question: How should worship be understood beyond ritual reduction, while still respecting the primary religious meaning?

### Hadith of Jibril / Sahih Muslim 8a — Islam, iman, ihsan
Research question: How does ihsan function as excellence under divine sight?

## Historical research tracks

### 1. Trust and commerce
- Market regulation.
- Honest weights and measures.
- Contract norms.
- Reputation and merchant ethics.

### 2. Stewardship and public good
- Waqf institutions.
- Hospitals.
- Schools and libraries.
- Water, roads, and public infrastructure.

### 3. Knowledge as responsibility
- Translation movements.
- Adab traditions.
- Scientific, legal, medical, and philosophical institutions.
- Knowledge transmission across regions and centuries.

### 4. Animal welfare and mercy
- Norms around treatment of animals.
- Legal and ethical discussions.
- Historical examples suitable for public education.

### 5. Multiple Islamic golden ages
Avoid treating the Islamic Golden Age as one frozen period. Track regional and temporal waves across Baghdad, Cordoba, Cairo, Damascus, Bukhara, Samarkand, Isfahan, Istanbul, Timbuktu, Mughal India, and other centers where source-backed evidence supports it.

## Guardrails
- Separate Qur'an, hadith, tafsir, fiqh, adab, philosophy, and history.
- Do not overstate continuity or perfection.
- Include failures, contradictions, decline dynamics, and regional variation.
- Avoid nostalgia. Extract operating principles without mythologizing.
- Source every historical claim before turning it into public content.
