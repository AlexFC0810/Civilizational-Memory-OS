# Civilizational System Models

## Seed → System → Fruit
- Seed: moral vision
- System: incentives, institutions, constraints
- Fruit: historical outputs

Judge systems by outputs over time, not claims alone.

## Calibration Gap
Most people are historically uncalibrated, not malicious.
Timeline facts help recalibrate.

## Power Gradient Test
Evaluate treatment of:
- animals
- poor
- children
- rulers
- enemies
- dissenters

## Invisible Ethics
How does a system behave when no one is watching?

## Human Failure Assumption
Strong systems assume:
- power can corrupt
- desire can escalate
- incentives matter

## Inner Transformation + Outer Constraint
Durable systems combine internal ethics with external guardrails.

## Constraint vs Freedom
Systems differ in assumptions about human self-regulation.

## Moral Compression
Strong systems encode memorable ethics that survive scale.

## Fruit-to-Claim Ratio
Claims require validation through outcomes.
