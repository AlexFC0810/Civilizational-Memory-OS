# Specialist Heavy-Lifting Protocol

## Purpose

Prevent the system from producing generic, cautious, or independently improvised defenses when a field specialist has already performed the decisive source work.

> **Do not rebuild a cathedral with a pocketknife. Find the scholar who already excavated the foundations, inspect the work directly, and convert the strongest defensible finding into advocacy.**

## Governing rule

Before drafting a high- or maximum-blast-radius defense, the system must identify and directly inspect the strongest specialist work that addresses the exact evidentiary bottleneck.

The specialist is not an authority substitute. Credentials do not settle the claim. The value is the work performed:

- primary-source collection;
- manuscript or edition control;
- transmission mapping;
- chronology reconstruction;
- philological analysis;
- dataset construction;
- comparative legal analysis;
- historical-context reconstruction;
- direct engagement with the strongest countercase.

## Required sequence

1. **Name the decisive bottleneck.**
   - What must be proved or disproved?
   - Is the dispute textual, historical, legal, clinical, statistical, or moral?
2. **Map the expert field.**
   - strongest supporting specialist;
   - strongest opposing specialist;
   - primary-source editor or dataset owner;
   - traditional/internal authority where relevant;
   - independent methodological critic.
3. **Retrieve before drafting.**
   - Directly inspect the monograph, dissertation, article, critical edition, dataset, or expert transcript.
   - A bibliography entry or secondhand summary is not inspection.
4. **Extract the actual heavy lifting.**
   - method;
   - corpus or dataset;
   - central finding;
   - confidence language;
   - limits;
   - falsifiers;
   - strongest rebuttal.
5. **Translate, do not merely name-drop.**
   - Explain what the expert discovered and why it changes the burden.
   - Do not rely on “Dr. X says…” without the evidentiary chain.
6. **Deploy asymmetrically.**
   - Lead with the strongest expert-grounded defense.
   - Make the accuser answer the analysis rather than merely repeat the allegation.
7. **Preserve disagreement.**
   - Do not call one specialist conclusion consensus unless independently established.
   - Include the strongest expert countercase internally and source-close it before maximum-blast-radius deployment.

## Specialist leverage test

A dossier fails if:

- a directly relevant specialist work exists but is only placed in a future-reading list;
- the response uses generic uncertainty instead of the specialist’s concrete analysis;
- the expert is cited only by credential or prestige;
- the method and source corpus are not described;
- the conclusion is strengthened beyond the expert’s own confidence;
- no serious opposing specialist is identified;
- the creator-facing answer makes the audience perform the technical synthesis themselves.

## Advocacy conversion

The expert packet must produce:

1. **one decisive factual sentence;**
2. **one burden-shifting question;**
3. **one 15-second answer;**
4. **one complete defense;**
5. **one clip-proof verdict;**
6. **one truth boundary;**
7. **one named counterexpert or unresolved challenge.**

## Example: Aisha’s age

Weak:

> “The report is canonical but historically contested.”

Strong:

> “The age is absent from the Qur’an. Joshua Little’s 546-page Oxford study collated every available version and concluded that they likely descend from a report put into circulation by Hisham ibn Urwah in eighth-century Iraq, not from independent eyewitness lines reaching Aisha. If you claim nine is proven history, rebut the provenance analysis.”

The improvement comes not from stronger adjectives but from importing the specialist’s method, corpus, finding, and burden shift.
