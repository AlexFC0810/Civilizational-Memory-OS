# Founder–Architect–Agent Division of Labor

**Status:** Canonical operating model
**Binding (2026-07-11):** this document defines *roles* (who does what kind of work). The *measured* model-tier assignment for each role lives in `/MODEL_ROUTING.md` ("Role mapping" section) and is authoritative — tiers move only on re-measured agreement data, not assertion.

## Core principle

The founder should not become the repository’s manual research engine. The founder’s highest leverage is signal generation, anomaly detection, moral seriousness, and selection of civilization-level questions.

The system should assign each kind of intelligence to the work it performs best.

## Founder role — signal and judgment

The founder:

- notices anomalies before they are formally legible;
- surfaces questions others do not ask;
- detects when a frame feels false, weak, or morally incomplete;
- connects distant domains;
- chooses which problems are load-bearing;
- evaluates whether the emerging synthesis has explanatory and moral force;
- protects the mission from becoming sterile scholarship or tribal propaganda.

The founder is the **high-signal catalyst**, not the citation bottleneck.

## AI architect role — map and ontology

The AI architect:

- turns exploration into durable frameworks;
- maps the full terrain and domain interfaces;
- atomizes claims;
- creates causal models and comparison classes;
- identifies hidden assumptions and missing questions;
- sequences research for maximum reuse;
- converts insights into packet templates, indexes, ledgers, and integration rules;
- preserves the golden thread across conversations.

The AI architect is the **cartographer and systems designer**.

## Scout-agent role — bounded evidence acquisition

Codex, Claude Code, deep-research agents, and human researchers:

- execute one bounded research packet;
- retrieve and classify sources;
- build tables, timelines, datasets, and manuscript or legal comparisons;
- distinguish source layers;
- document uncertainty;
- search for counterevidence;
- return structured findings without independently rewriting canon.

Scout agents are **expeditions**, not sovereign editors.

## Red-team role — epistemic immune system

Red-team agents:

- attack favored conclusions;
- search for breaker evidence;
- identify circularity, mistranslation, selection bias, and missing cases;
- test whether the comparison is symmetrical;
- detect apologetic escape hatches and hostile framing tricks;
- determine which claims should be narrowed, rejected, or deferred.

The red team is not opposition to the mission. It is protection from self-deception.

## Integration role — evidence compiler

The integration layer:

- reconciles scout and red-team findings;
- updates evidence graphs;
- proposes versioned verdicts;
- prevents contradictory canon;
- records what changed and why;
- preserves rejected and superseded claims;
- routes findings into frameworks, claim cards, benchmarks, datasets, and public narratives.

## Editor role — truthful compression

The editor:

- converts accepted synthesis into memorable language;
- preserves caveats that materially affect truth;
- refuses brittle certainty;
- distinguishes internal research language from public language;
- ensures no rhetorical line outruns its evidence.

## Anti-bottleneck rules

1. The founder does not need to know the perfect source before raising a high-signal question.
2. The AI architect does not need to perform every deep literature review before mapping the research cell.
3. Scout agents may not silently expand scope.
4. Raw volume may not substitute for source quality.
5. No agent may canonize its own conclusion.
6. The red team must be structurally independent from the scout’s desired verdict.
7. Integration must preserve unresolved disagreement.
8. Public content must never become the only surviving record of a claim.

## Operating loop

`Founder signal -> Architect map -> Packet assignment -> Scout evidence -> Red-team attack -> Integration verdict -> Editor compression -> Reality feedback -> Map revision`

## Highest-leverage allocation

When the project feels stuck, ask:

- Is the question missing, or is the evidence missing?
- Is the terrain unmapped, or is a mapped cell unresearched?
- Is the evidence contradictory, or merely unintegrated?
- Is the claim unresolved, or simply overcompressed?
- Does the founder need to decide, or can the decision be converted into an explicit benchmark?

## Canonical synthesis

> The founder generates signal. The architect creates the map. Scouts gather evidence. Red teams search for failure. Integrators create versioned knowledge. Editors compress only what survives.

> Human vision should direct the expedition without becoming the pack animal carrying every source.
