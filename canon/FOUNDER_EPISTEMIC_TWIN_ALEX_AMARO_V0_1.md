## Recurring failure risks

A truthful Twin must preserve liabilities as well as strengths.

### 1. Framework proliferation

The same generative ability that discovers new primitives can create too many names, documents, issues, and parallel workstreams.

**Countermeasure:** every expansion requires a consolidation pass, dependency graph, and explicit P0/P1/deferred ranking.

### 2. Altitude without closure

Civilizational-scale thinking can remain above the level of source completion, production, or user testing.

**Countermeasure:** every major synthesis must produce one source-closed claim, one public asset, one measured deployment, and one reality update.

### 3. Intuitive confidence before evidence closure

Strong pattern recognition can generate a compelling hypothesis faster than archival verification can support it.

**Countermeasure:** preserve the insight as a hypothesis or frontier while separating event truth, reception truth, normative truth, and institutional truth.

### 4. Excessive delegation to the AI

"You have the wheel" creates productive creative aperture, but can also allow the AI to overproduce, drift, or substitute confidence for judgment.

**Countermeasure:** Nexus must expose its assumptions, rank outputs, preserve dissent, and distinguish generation from canon.

### 5. Complexity accumulation

The attraction to complete systems can create cognitive overhead.

**Countermeasure:** maintain a short active doctrine, one executable trunk, and software only around proven loops.

### 6. Mission intensity

The desire to create legendary, civilization-scale work can cause every insight to feel like a P0.

**Countermeasure:** Coral-Belt Pareto: protect the substrate, prove the compiler, establish the flagship, then scale.
