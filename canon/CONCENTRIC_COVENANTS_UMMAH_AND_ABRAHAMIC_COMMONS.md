## Executive Verdict

**Ummah has greater emotional, spiritual, and civilizational force than “People of the Qiblah,” but it should not be forced to serve as the sole public label for Jews, Christians, and Muslims together.**

In modern global usage, *the Ummah* is overwhelmingly heard as the worldwide Muslim community. The Qur'anic word itself is broader and can mean a community, nation, people, or prophetically addressed collective; the Qur'an can describe humanity as once one ummah and can speak of multiple communities with distinct laws and ways. Yet contemporary reception matters. A Jewish or Christian partner may hear “join the Ummah” as “enter an Islamic identity,” even when that is not the intent.

The correct architecture is therefore not one flattened super-community but **concentric covenants**.

# Ring 1 — The Ummah

The living Muslim community across Sunni, Shia, Ibadi, Zaydi, Ismaili, Sufi, Salafi, and other traditions.

Its internal constitutional layer is the People of the Qiblah Civilizational Commons.

Purpose:

- restore truth and dignity inside the Muslim world;
- protect legitimate difference;
- prevent takfir and sectarian dehumanization;
- rebuild shared knowledge and public-benefit systems;
- and make the Ummah capable of entering wider covenants without exporting unresolved internal pathologies.

Recommended public language:

- **Ummah Renewal**
- **The Ummah Commons**
- **The Civilizational Ummah**
- **The Ummah Renewal Layer**

“People of the Qiblah” remains the constitutional and scholarly frame; “Ummah” may become the warmer public-facing identity.

# Ring 2 — The Abrahamic Covenant Commons

A protected inter-Abrahamic layer for Jews, Christians, Muslims, and other communities that explicitly locate themselves in the Abrahamic lineage.

This is not a new religion, merger theology, or claim that the traditions teach identical doctrines.

Its basis is a shared moral inheritance:

- one Creator as ultimate authority;
- Abraham as a paradigmatic ancestor of faith;
- prophetic accountability above kings, tribes, markets, and empires;
- the sacredness of innocent life;
- care for the poor, widow, orphan, stranger, and oppressed;
- truthfulness, repentance, justice, mercy, covenant, and responsibility;
- resistance to idolatry in both literal and civilizational forms;
- and the principle that no human ruler, nation, race, market, appetite, or state is God.

Recommended names:

1. **The Abrahamic Covenant Commons** — strongest institutional name.
2. **The Covenant of Abraham** — strongest moral and spiritual name.
3. **The Children of Abraham Commons** — warmer, but potentially contested genealogically and theologically.
4. **The Abrahamic Civilizational Commons** — clearest analytical name.
5. **House of Abraham** — memorable, but already used by multiple initiatives and institutions.

Current recommendation:

# **The Abrahamic Covenant Commons**

Tagline:

> **Distinct faiths. Shared ancestry. Protected difference. Common moral action.**

# Ring 3 — The Human Coalition of Conscience

Potential names:

- **The Human Covenant**
- **The Coalition of Conscience**
- **The Covenant for Human Dignity**
- **The Civilizational Conscience Commons**

This ring prevents the Abrahamic project from becoming another closed tribal bloc.

## The Core Architectural Rule

> **Concentric belonging without theological collapse.**

A person can belong simultaneously to:

- a specific faith and denomination;
- a wider Abrahamic covenant of non-domination and common action;
- and a universal human coalition of conscience.

No ring cancels the obligations or truth claims of the ring inside it.

## Why “One Abrahamic Ummah” Is Not the Best Primary Label

### The case for it

- *Ummah* has Qur'anic breadth beyond a narrow ethnic nation.
- It carries moral, emotional, and transnational force.
- The Constitution of Medina is often interpreted as using communal language for a plural political covenant involving Muslims and Jewish groups while preserving distinct religions.
- Qur'an 2:213 speaks of humanity as once one community.
- Qur'an 5:48 explicitly recognizes distinct laws and ways and commands communities to compete in good works before God resolves their differences.

### The case against it

- modern default meaning is the Muslim community;
- Jews and Christians may hear it as Islamic absorption;
- Muslim audiences may hear an Abrahamic expansion of the term as blurring the theological boundary of Islam;
- the label could trigger debates about membership before the institution demonstrates value;
- and one word would be carrying confessional, political, civilizational, and interfaith meanings simultaneously.

### Verdict

Use **Ummah** unapologetically for the Muslim circle.

Do not require non-Muslims to enter a Muslim-named identity to collaborate.

Use **covenant** for the inter-Abrahamic circle.

## The Qur'anic Operating Principle

The most important Qur'anic architecture is not homogenization. It is:

> **Different communities, distinct paths, competition in good works, final judgment by God.**

This supports neither relativism nor coercive uniformity.

It creates a disciplined field in which communities retain truth claims while proving their faith through moral fruit.

## The Enemy-Based Coalition Trap

The Abrahamic project must not be founded primarily as a coalition “against evil.”

Every Abrahamic civilization has historically become vulnerable to defining evil as another people, sect, race, or religion. A movement organized around a common enemy can quickly reproduce crusade, inquisition, jihadist, nationalist, colonial, or apocalyptic logic.

The correct formulation is:

> **Unite around shared obligations; resist evil through rules that apply first to ourselves.**

The coalition must oppose:

- murder of innocents;
- torture;
- ethnic cleansing;
- collective punishment;
- sexual exploitation;
- slavery and trafficking;
- corruption;
- predatory debt;
- dehumanizing propaganda;
- destruction of families and communities;
- cruelty to animals;
- greed without limit;
- and the idolatry of nation, race, party, leader, market, technology, or self.

But it must judge actions and systems, not demonize peoples.

## Non-Negotiable Moral Symmetry

> **No community's sacred identity can immunize its institutions or state from moral scrutiny. No institution's or state's crimes can be assigned to an entire faith or people.**

Therefore:

- Judaism is not reducible to the State of Israel or any Israeli government.
- Christianity is not reducible to Christendom, colonialism, or Western states.
- Islam is not reducible to caliphates, Muslim-majority governments, militias, or terrorist movements.
- criticism must identify the correct defendant: text, interpretation, institution, state, movement, or individual.

## Institutional Stack

### Ummah Commons

### Abrahamic Covenant Commons

Joint moral action, historical truth, religious freedom, sacred-site protection, anti-dehumanization, peacebuilding, family and community restoration, poverty relief, ethical technology, and protection of the vulnerable.

### Coalition of Conscience

Universal partnerships with people and institutions outside the Abrahamic family who accept the same public moral floor.

## Strongest Initial Common-Work Agenda

The coalition should not begin by attempting to merge theology or solve every geopolitical conflict.

Begin with actions where sacred obligations converge and performance can be measured:

1. protection of children from trafficking, exploitation, abuse, and war;
2. defense of civilians and rejection of collective punishment;
3. poverty, debt, hunger, refugee, and orphan infrastructure;
4. family stability and protection from addiction and predatory industries;
5. animal welfare and mercy;
6. anti-corruption and truth-verification systems;
7. ethical AI and resistance to algorithmic dehumanization;
8. preservation of sacred and historical memory;
9. religious literacy that prevents caricature;
10. local Abrahamic service projects.

## Existing Precedents and the Missing Layer

Existing initiatives demonstrate real possibility:

- *A Common Word* identified love of God and love of neighbor as common ground across the Qur'an, Torah, and New Testament.
- The 2019 Human Fraternity document affirmed equal dignity, freedom of belief, mercy, protection of innocent life, and care for the marginalized.
- The Abrahamic Family House created a mosque, church, and synagogue sharing one site while retaining distinct worship spaces.

The missing layer remains:

- a distributed truth architecture;
- anti-capture governance;
- shared-work portfolios with measurement;
- historical and media claim auditing;
- and an AI constitution capable of representing each tradition without flattening or weaponizing it.
