## 7. Canonical terms

### Civilizational Mercy

The capacity of a civilization to preserve moral visibility and enforce responsibility toward beings beneath concentrated power.

### Voiceless Covenant

A being’s inability to speak, vote, testify, organize, own property, or retaliate strengthens rather than weakens the duty of protection.

### Distance–Innocence Machine

A system that separates beneficiary, decision-maker, operator, and victim until organized harm feels authorless.

### Digital Amphitheater

A network in which the device becomes witness, stage, status mechanism, distribution system, and possible market for cruelty.

### Bismillah Test

Can an action honestly be undertaken in the name of the Most Merciful?

### Mercy Stack

Tawḥīd → ontology → raḥmah → story → ritual → taqwā → amānah → iḥsān → law → institution → correction → judgment.

### Group-power stack

ʿAsabiyyah → raḥmah → Sharīʿah → taqwā → institution → correction.
