# Frameworks Index

This folder contains reusable thinking systems for Civilizational Memory OS.

The goal is not to store isolated insights. The goal is to create compounding frameworks that can be reused across religion, history, politics, ideology, civilization, ethics, and future strategy.

---

## Current core frameworks

### 1. Seed vs Corruption Framework

**File:** `frameworks/seed-vs-corruption-framework.md`

Use this when a critique collapses original moral insight into later human distortion.

Core question:

> Is this attacking the seed, or the corruption around the seed?

Best for:

- Christianity vs Christendom;
- Islam vs dynastic empires;
- Judaism vs nationalist capture;
- liberalism vs technocratic/imperial capture;
- socialism vs authoritarian capture;
- any reform movement that becomes institution.

---

### 2. Domination vs Accountability Framework

**File:** `frameworks/domination-vs-accountability.md`

Use this to reframe civilizational history around the central struggle between power without judgment and power under moral constraint.

Core question:

> What system best restrains domination and protects the vulnerable when compassionate people are not in power?

Best for:

- religion vs secularism debates;
- empire analysis;
- law and punishment;
- hidden evil / psychopath problem;
- anti-idolatry thesis;
- moral operating systems of civilization.

---

### 3. Comparative Civilization Moral Index

**File:** `frameworks/comparative-civilization-moral-index.md`

Use this to benchmark civilizations relative to historical baseline instead of abstract perfection.

Core question:

> What did this civilization restrain, protect, normalize, fail to correct, and set in motion?

Best for comparing:

- hunter-gatherer societies;
- Mesopotamia;
- Egypt;
- Israelite prophetic tradition;
- Greece/Rome;
- Christianity;
- Islam;
- Buddhism/Ashoka;
- Confucian China;
- Enlightenment liberalism;
- socialism;
- modern secular technocracy.

### 4. Criticism Matrix Template

**File:** `frameworks/criticism-matrix-template.md`

Use this to turn objections into structured inquiry.

Best for:

- Abrahamic criticism atlas;
- apologetics audits;
- atheistic polemics;
- ideology critiques;
- social media debate analysis;

## Operating sequence

When a major conversation produces insights:

1. Log the full conversation synthesis under `conversation-logs/`.
2. Extract reusable frameworks into `frameworks/`.
3. Extract unverifiable or citation-dependent claims into `source-ledgers/`.
4. Create future research tasks from unresolved tensions.
5. Reuse the frameworks in future threads instead of rediscovering them from scratch.

## Current golden thread

> Human beings are morally variable. Civilizations create power at scale. Power creates domination unless restrained. The highest moral operating systems are those that protect the vulnerable, expose hidden evil, place power under accountability, and correct their own corruption.
