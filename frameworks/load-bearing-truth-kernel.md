## Purpose

Civilizational truth work can drown in facts, trivia, quotations, clips, and reactive rebuttals.

The objective is not to remember everything.

It is to identify the small set of truths, distinctions, questions, and source rules that carry most of the explanatory weight.

> **Pareto selects the highest-leverage truths. First principles strips claims back to source, definition, mechanism, and evidence.**

The result is a **Load-Bearing Truth Kernel**: the minimum durable knowledge required to prevent a large family of downstream misconceptions.

This is not a slogan bank. It is a compression and retrieval system for truth.

---

## Core thesis

A debate should not be won by whoever has the fastest memory, loudest voice, or strongest stage presence.

A well-designed truth system should make the best available answer retrievable even by a sincere non-expert under pressure.

> **Truth should not depend on whether the person defending it happens to be the better debater.**

The engine therefore converts large research fields into compact, source-calibrated kernels that preserve uncertainty and valid criticism.

## The three-axis distinction

The most important first-principles upgrade is to separate three questions that are often collapsed.

### Axis 1 — Normative authority

For a Qur'an-first Islamic inquiry:

- What does the Qur'an explicitly say?
- What principles does it establish?
- What does it not say?
- Which later claims are being attributed to it without textual warrant?

The Qur'an is the normative root for this project.

### Axis 2 — Historical probability

Historical questions require a different evidence test:

- How early is the source?
- Is it independent?
- Is it multiply attested?
- Can transmission development be reconstructed?
- Does chronology cohere?
- What are the uncertainties?

The Qur'an may be the normative authority while remaining silent about a particular biographical detail.

### Axis 3 — Implementation and outcome

A government, jurist, movement, culture, family, or individual may:

- implement the normative source well;
- misunderstand it;
- selectively apply it;
- combine it with custom;
- weaponize it;
- violate it while retaining the label.

> **Normative authority, historical probability, and implementation are related—but they are not interchangeable.**

This distinction prevents two errors:

1. treating every Muslim act as a Qur'anic command;
2. treating the Qur'an as a comprehensive historical chronicle of events it never specifies.

## The source ladder

Every consequential claim must be assigned to a source layer before evaluation.

| Layer | Source type | Primary question |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Qur'anic text | What is explicitly stated, implied, or absent? |
| 1 | contemporaneous documentary or material evidence | What can be established closest to the event? |
| 2 | early independent historical reports | How early, independent, and coherent are they? |
| 3 | hadith, sira, and biographical transmission | What is the transmission history and confidence? |
| 4 | tafsir, jurisprudence, and later doctrine | How did interpreters convert sources into rules? |
| 5 | state law and institutional policy | Who enacted what, where, and when? |
| 6 | culture, custom, movement, or individual conduct | Is this representative, authorized, resisted, or deviant? |
| 7 | modern commentary, polemic, clip, or meme | What earlier layer is actually being cited? |

No claim may silently jump layers.

> **Name the claim. Name the layer. Name the century.**

## What makes a truth load-bearing?

A truth is load-bearing when learning it changes many downstream judgments.

Score each candidate 1–5 on:

- **coverage:** how many misconceptions depend on it;
- **causal depth:** whether it corrects the underlying model rather than one example;
- **retrievability:** whether a person can remember and use it under pressure;
- **transfer:** whether it applies to a new case;
- **evidence strength:** whether it survives adversarial review;
- **moral importance:** whether error causes dehumanization, injustice, or preventable harm.

High-scoring items enter the kernel. Low-scoring trivia stays in the research layer.

## The SCOPE loop

Use **SCOPE** to answer a live claim without being dragged into a gish gallop.

### S — State the exact claim

Do not answer an atmosphere, insinuation, or ten claims at once.

> “Are you claiming the Qur'an says this, that later jurists said it, or that some Muslims practiced it?”

### C — Classify the source

Identify Qur'an, hadith, history, jurisprudence, state, custom, or individual.

### O — Observe the full evidence

Include:

- strongest supporting evidence;
- strongest opposing evidence;
- chronology;
- source independence;
- comparison class;
- denominator;
- uncertainty.

### P — Preserve valid criticism

Do not use source separation as an escape hatch.

A claim can be misattributed to the Qur'an while still identifying a real juristic tradition, abusive law, or recurring institutional failure.

### E — Express the calibrated verdict

Use one of:

- established;
- strongly supported;
- probable;
- plausible;
- disputed;
- weak;
- unsupported;
- false as stated;
- unknown.

Then give the shortest accurate answer that preserves the verdict.

## Required answer layers

### 15-second kernel

One verdict, one distinction, one question.

### 60-second answer

Add the source ladder, strongest evidence, and confidence boundary.

### 5-minute answer

Add history, counterevidence, valid criticism, and the implementation gap.

The short answer must be a compression of the long answer—not a more confident or distorted answer.

## Debate execution rules

1. Freeze one claim before answering.
2. Make the claimant identify the source.
3. Do not defend a quotation that is not in the Qur'an as though it were.
4. Do not deny hadith, jurisprudence, conquest, abuse, or state policy merely because it is not Qur'anic.
5. Distinguish a historical possibility from a demonstrated fact.
6. State uncertainty before the opponent exposes it.
7. Refuse reciprocal collective blame.
8. Never use another civilization's failure as proof that every Islamic implementation is sound.
9. Preserve victims and valid criticism.
10. End with a transferable question, not only a rebuttal.

## The adversarial-verse gate

A Qur'an-first project loses credibility if it cites only easy verses.

For each claim family:

- identify the strongest verse used by critics;
- inspect the Arabic terms and immediate context;
- map major interpretive traditions;
- distinguish what the verse can and cannot establish;
- preserve morally difficult implications;
- reject euphemistic translation games;
- state unresolved questions.

> **A single source of truth still requires honest interpretation.**

---

## The hard-case standard

A kernel is not ready when it only persuades supporters.

It must survive:

- a traditional Sunni critic;
- a traditional Shia critic;
- a Qur'an-only critic;
- a secular historian;
- a hostile anti-Muslim polemicist;
- a Muslim woman affected by the rule;
- a victim of institutional abuse;
- a cold audience with no prior context.

Disagreement must be represented rather than erased.

---

## Success criteria

The framework succeeds when a user can encounter a novel claim and independently ask:

1. What exactly is being claimed?
2. Where does it come from?
3. What does the Qur'an actually say?
4. What historical evidence exists?
5. What later interpretation or implementation is being conflated with the source?
6. What valid criticism remains?
7. How certain should I be?
8. What evidence would change my mind?

The goal is not merely better Muslim debaters.

It is a population that can detect source substitution, false certainty, temporal collapse, and collective blame in real time.
