# Qur'an-First, Full-Record Response Protocol — V1

> **STATUS: NONCANONICAL FRAMEWORK PROPOSAL**  
> This file defines a source-ordering and advocacy method. It does not canonize any historical claim merely because it appears here.

## Core doctrine

> **Return to the root, then reopen the record.**

For claims about what Islam commands, requires, praises, condemns, or makes normative, the Qur'an is the controlling canonical authority in this project.

Other sources remain important, but they must wear name tags:

- **Qur'an:** controlling normative text for Qur'an-first claims;
- **hadith:** transmitted reports with collection, chain, date, grading, variants, and interpretive history;
- **sīrah and chronicles:** historical testimony and reconstruction;
- **fiqh:** human jurisprudential reasoning;
- **state law and ruler conduct:** implementation evidence, not revelation;
- **culture and individual conduct:** sociological evidence, not divine command;
- **comparative history:** context, baseline, causation, and performance.

Compact formula:

> **One canonical authority. Plural evidence. Visible inference. Permanent correction.**

## Legal analogy

The House of Wisdom should think like counsel:

- the **Qur'an is the controlling text**;
- hadith and chronicles are forms of **testimony and transmitted evidence**;
- fiqh is a body of **human legal interpretation**;
- government policy is **state implementation**;
- history is the **record of performance and failure**.

A later witness may illuminate the controlling text. A jurist may interpret it. A ruler may obey or violate it. None may silently rewrite it.

> **Do not let a hostile witness rewrite the governing text.**

## The seven-step response

### 1. Freeze the accusation

State the exact proposition being alleged.

Do not answer an emotional cloud such as “Islam is barbaric.”

Ask:

> **“What exactly are you saying Islam commands, and what source are you attributing it to?”**

### 2. Audit the Qur'an

Classify the claim as:

- `QURAN_EXPLICIT`;
- `QURAN_INFERENCE`;
- `QURAN_CONTRARY`;
- `QURAN_SILENT`;
- `QURAN_DISPUTED_TRANSLATION`.

Questions:

- Is the proposition actually stated?
- Is the Qur'an silent?
- Does the Qur'an establish a broader principle that cuts against the smear?
- Is a later source being inserted into the Qur'an?

### 3. Present the Qur'anic architecture

Do not use one isolated verse when the governing pattern requires several.

Show:

- the moral principle;
- the legal or economic mechanism;
- repeated reinforcement;
- any material boundary.

### 4. Label the later source

If a hadith, jurist, ruler, or custom is relevant, identify it precisely.

Use language such as:

> “A report in Sahih al-Bukhari says…”

not:

> “The Qur'an says…”

Hadith need not be discarded to preserve Qur'an-first authority. They must be named as hadith rather than authority-laundered into revelation.

### 5. Establish the historical baseline

Ask:

> **“What world did the Qur'an enter?”**

Then compare like with like:

- same century;
- same category;
- actual contemporaries;
- similar political and material conditions.

Avoid universal overclaims such as “every civilization” when “many major ancient and premodern societies” is the accurate statement.

### 6. Audit implementation

Ask whether Muslims, jurists, rulers, markets, and institutions activated, narrowed, ignored, or betrayed the architecture.

> **Blueprint ≠ interpretation ≠ implementation ≠ historical fruit.**

The failure of Muslims to implement an exit mechanism does not erase the mechanism. The existence of the mechanism does not prove universal implementation.

### 7. Deliver the advocacy package

Use:

- strongest fact;
- load-bearing question;
- verdict;
- private defense note.

Public structure:

> **Root → Record → Question → Verdict**

## Example 1 — Slavery and manumission

### Accusation

> “Islam encouraged slavery.”

### Historical baseline

Slavery and unfree labor were deeply embedded across many major ancient and premodern societies. Do not claim every society depended on slavery in the same way or that it was always the economic backbone.

The accurate comparative opening is:

> **“The Qur'an did not enter a world debating whether slavery should be invented. It entered a world in which slavery was already deeply institutionalized across major civilizations.”**

### Qur'anic architecture

The Qur'an repeatedly directs resources and moral prestige toward liberation:

- **2:177:** freeing captives or bondspeople is included in righteousness;
- **9:60:** obligatory alms include a category for freeing people from bondage;
- **24:33:** people in bondage seeking a freedom contract are to be contracted with and given financial help; the same verse forbids forcing bondwomen into prostitution;
- **4:92:** freeing a person from bondage is an expiation for accidental killing;
- **58:3:** freeing a person from bondage is an expiation for a serious marital wrong;
- **90:13:** freeing a person is named as part of the steep moral path.

“Manumission engine” is a synthesis term, not a Qur'anic phrase. It describes the combination of:

- moral praise;
- public funding;
- expiation;
- contractual self-purchase;
- required financial assistance;
- protection from sexual coercion.

### Source-safe public answer

> **“Slavery was not an Islamic invention; it was embedded across the premodern world. The distinctive Qur'anic question is what the text does inside that world. It makes freeing people righteousness, directs public charity toward liberation, turns manumission into expiation for serious wrongs, and orders freedom contracts with financial help. That is not encouragement of slavery. It is an architecture of exits from an inherited institution.”**

### Load-bearing question

> **“What scripture accused of encouraging slavery makes liberation righteousness, public expenditure, repentance, and a contractual path to freedom?”**

### Verdict

> **“The Qur'an inherited a slave world and installed multiple exits from it. Muslim history must then be judged by whether Muslims activated or obstructed those exits.”**

### Boundary

Do not claim the Qur'an issued a single universal statutory abolition decree. Do not let that fact erase its repeated liberation architecture.

## Example 2 — Dogs and the animal moral community

### Accusation

> “Muslims hate dogs.”

### Qur'anic architecture

The Qur'an does not present animals as spiritually empty objects:

- **6:38:** land animals and birds are communities like human communities;
- **17:44:** all things glorify God, though humans may not understand their glorification;
- **24:41:** creatures, including birds, know their manner of prayer and glorification;
- **5:4:** trained hunting animals are treated as legitimate partners in obtaining food;
- **18:18 and 18:22:** the Companions of the Cave are repeatedly narrated with “their dog.”

The Qur'an contains no general command that Muslims hate dogs and no general dog-ownership prohibition.

### Hadith layer

A separately labeled report in Sahih al-Bukhari 3321 and Sahih Muslim 2245a says a prostitute was forgiven or pardoned after drawing water for a desperately thirsty dog.

Use the exact source-safe claim:

> **“The report says she was forgiven or pardoned.”**

Do not automatically embellish it into “the report explicitly guarantees paradise” unless citing a narration that says so.

### Source-safe public answer

> **“No—the Qur'an does not teach hatred of dogs. It calls animals communities like us, says creation glorifies God in ways we may not understand, permits trained hunting animals, and even narrates the faithful sleepers with their dog. Then, in a separately identified hadith layer, a woman is forgiven for saving a thirsty dog. Later purity and household rulings may be debated as hadith and fiqh; they cannot be smuggled into the Qur'an as a command to hate an animal.”**

### Load-bearing question

> **“Where is the Qur'anic command to hate dogs—and how does that claim explain animals as communities, trained hunting partners, the Cave dog, and a mercy tradition in which giving water to a dog becomes grounds for forgiveness?”**

### Verdict

> **“A purity ruling is not hatred. A later juristic restriction is not a Qur'anic moral verdict on an entire species.”**
