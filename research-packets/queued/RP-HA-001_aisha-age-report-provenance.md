## Exact question

What is the provenance, transmission history, chronological coherence, and historical reliability of reports that Aisha was six at betrothal and nine at consummation?

## Why this matters

The claim is routinely presented either as unquestionable history or as completely debunked. The packet must determine what is established, contested, inferential, or unknown without allowing moral preference to decide authenticity.

## Claim atoms

1. Major Sunni collections contain six/nine reports.
2. The reports trace through identifiable transmission pathways.
3. Hisham b. Urwa is central or non-central to the report complex.
4. The reports circulated first or most strongly in Iraq rather than Medina.
5. Alternative chronologies based on Asma, migration, battle participation, or other reports support an older age.
6. Those alternative chronologies are themselves reliable enough to bear weight.
7. Later sectarian, legal, or biographical incentives may explain the report’s formation or spread.
8. Acceptance in canonical collections establishes normative authority, historical probability, both, or neither.
9. Even acceptance of the age report does or does not justify modern psychiatric labeling.

## Mandatory source classes

- full Arabic and translated versions of the relevant hadith reports;
- isnad maps across collections;
- early sira and tabaqat chronologies;
- Joshua Little’s Oxford thesis and any published follow-up;
- traditional hadith defenses;
- specialist critical responses to revisionist claims;
- demographic and marriage-context scholarship only as contextual evidence, never as proof of age.

## Required deliverables

1. complete report-family table;
2. isnad/common-link visualization;

4. alternative chronology table with dependency risks;
5. strongest traditional case;
6. strongest revisionist case;
7. separate verdicts for:
   - canonical status;

   - validity of the “pedophile” label;
8. prohibited overclaims for both sides.

## Breaker evidence

Examples that could materially change the verdict:

- an independently transmitted early Medinan attestation predating the proposed Iraqi spread;
- evidence that the apparent single-source structure is an artifact of incomplete surviving records;
- a decisive chronological anchor establishing Aisha’s birth year;
- a major methodological flaw in the source-critical reconstruction;
- evidence that alternative-age chronologies depend on equally late or circular reports.
