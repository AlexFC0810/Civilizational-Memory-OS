# RP-SL-001 — Qur’anic Slavery Inflow/Outflow Model

- **Research theater:** Slavery and manumission
- **Parent issue:** #15
- **Status:** queued
- **Priority:** P0

## Exact question

What inflows into slavery, rights and constraints within slavery, and exits from slavery are present in the Qur’an itself; and, if modeled as a stock-and-flow system, does the Qur’anic architecture tend toward depletion, stabilization, or reproduction of the enslaved population?

## Why this matters

“Islam abolished slavery” and “Islam is simply pro-slavery” are both too blunt. This packet makes the manumission-engine hypothesis testable.

## Scope lock

Phase 1 is Qur’an-first. Later hadith, jurisprudence, and imperial practice must be placed in separate layers rather than silently merged.

## Required subquestions

### Inflows
1. Does the Qur’an itself authorize enslavement from war captivity, birth, purchase, debt, punishment, kidnapping, tribute, or any other channel?
2. Which possible inflows are explicit, inferred, or supplied only by later law?
3. How should Qur’an 47:4 be read in relation to captivity and later enslavement doctrines?

### Conditions inside the system
4. What moral, legal, sexual, economic, familial, and religious status does the Qur’an assign to enslaved people?
5. What protections exist against forced prostitution, abuse, family disruption, or economic exclusion?
6. What moral equality language applies, and what legal inequality remains?

### Outflows
7. Where is manumission praised, required, funded, contracted, or used as expiation?
8. What role do zakat and mukataba play?
9. Are exits mandatory, incentive-based, discretionary, or conditional?

### System dynamics
10. Under full compliance, would inflows exceed outflows, outflows exceed inflows, or is the answer indeterminate?
11. What additional assumptions are required to claim eventual abolition?
12. Where did later Muslim law or state practice replenish slavery beyond the narrowest Qur’anic reading?

## Mandatory evidence table

For every relevant verse record:

- Arabic key term;
- translation variants;
- immediate context;
- category: inflow / condition / outflow / ambiguous;
- legal force: command / permission / praise / description / expiation;
- major interpretive disputes;
- later juristic expansion or restriction.

## Comparison classes

Do not compare the Qur’anic blueprint directly to nineteenth-century abolition law alone. Include:

- late antique Roman/Byzantine systems;
- Sasanian context;
- biblical and rabbinic legal inheritance;
- early Islamic practice;
- Atlantic hereditary racial chattel slavery;
- modern abolition as a later benchmark.

## Required deliverables

1. machine-readable verse ledger;
2. stock-flow diagram;
3. minimum and maximum plausible inflow models;
4. exit-mechanism table;
5. “full compliance” counterfactual;
6. strongest abolitionary-vector case;
7. strongest critical case;
8. distinction between Qur’anic architecture, classical law, and historical practice;
9. confidence-scored verdict on “proto-abolition,” “abolitionary vector,” “transition architecture,” and “effective ban.”

## Breaker evidence

Evidence that could falsify or narrow the manumission-engine thesis includes:

- an explicit Qur’anic inflow large enough to structurally replenish the stock;
- evidence that key exit mechanisms were too discretionary or rare to create depletion;
- a strong textual basis for hereditary reproduction;
- proof that the proposed “full compliance” model depends mainly on later moral interpolation;
- evidence that Qur’an 47:4 does not constrain enslavement in the way proposed.
