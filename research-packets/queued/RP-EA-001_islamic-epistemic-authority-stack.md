# RP-EA-001 — Islamic Epistemic Authority Stack

- **Research theater:** Epistemic authority
- **Parent issue:** #17
- **Status:** queued
- **Priority:** P0

## Exact question

How should Qur’an, Sunnah, surviving hadith reports, sira, tafsir, fiqh, ijma, qiyas, urf, institutional practice, imperial practice, and modern interpretation be distinguished when attributing a proposition to “Islam”?

## Why this matters

Almost every major controversy collapses multiple authority layers into the phrase “Islam says.” This packet establishes the source grammar required for all later research.

## Required subquestions

1. What authority does the Qur’an claim for itself?
2. What does the Qur’an say about obedience to and following the Messenger?
3. How should lived Sunnah be distinguished from surviving individual hadith reports?
4. Which practices are mass-transmitted, and which depend on isolated reports?
5. How do historical probability and normative authority differ?
6. What can isnad criticism establish? What can matn, common-link, and source-critical methods add?
7. How do Sunni, Shia, Ibadi, Mu‘tazili, Quran-centric, modernist, and revisionist models differ?
8. What does ijma establish, and when can it reflect institutional lock-in rather than independent evidence?
9. What minimum language is required before saying “Islam teaches X”?

## Mandatory source classes

- Qur’anic passages concerning revelation, obedience, judgment, explanation, and the Messenger;
- early hadith and legal-method texts;
- specialist histories of hadith compilation and Islamic jurisprudence;
- traditional Sunni and Shia methodological sources;
- Quran-centric and modern source-critical scholarship;
- scholarship outside the dominant U.S. frame where relevant.

## Required deliverables

1. one-page authority-layer diagram;
2. glossary of core terms;
3. chronology of major collections and legal schools;
4. matrix separating historical probability from normative authority;
5. attribution-language standard;
6. strongest traditionalist case and strongest critical case;
7. worked examples: prayer, Aisha’s age, slavery/manumission, apostasy, stoning;
8. list of unresolved methodological disputes.

## Attribution-language standard to test

Every conclusion should use one of these forms:

- `The Qur’an explicitly states...`
- `A major hadith collection reports...`
- `This practice is mass-transmitted...`
- `Classical jurists commonly ruled...`
- `One legal school held...`
- `A particular empire practiced...`
- `A modern movement interprets...`
- `The surviving historical evidence suggests...`

## Breaker evidence

The packet must identify evidence that would undermine:

- the traditional claim that canonical hadith methods reliably preserve Prophetic Sunnah;
- the Quran-centric claim that the Qur’an alone supplies a practicable and historically coherent religious system;
- the assumption that later consensus independently verifies early history;
- the assumption that historical authenticity automatically establishes timeless normativity.
