>

> For each model, state:
>
> - what it explains;
> - what it does not explain;
> - its misuse risk;
> - what additional evidence or discipline should constrain it.
>
> ## Phase 4 — Stress-test the framework
>
> Apply the architecture carefully to at least four cases:
>

> - one Christian case;
> - one secular political or academic institution;

> - Could it become scholar worship?
> - Could “fruit” become crude consequentialism?
> - Could power analysis erase sincere belief?
> - Could root language become a weapon for anyone claiming to represent the “true essence”?

> - Truth Court internally; no protection of favored traditions from inconvenient evidence.
> - Never collapse later religious history into revelation.
> - Never assume later means corrupt or earlier means faithful.
> - Retrieve specialist labor before drafting high-blast-radius conclusions.

> Produce:

> 4. four comparative stress tests;
> 5. the strongest unresolved objections;

> End with this test:
>
> > **Can the institution, scholar, movement, or interpretation still be judged and corrected by the prophetic root—or has it made its own survival sacred?**
