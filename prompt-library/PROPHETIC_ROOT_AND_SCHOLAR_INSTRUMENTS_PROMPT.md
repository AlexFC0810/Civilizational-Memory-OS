# Prophetic Root and Scholar Instruments Prompt

**Status:** Reusable research bootstrap v0.1  
**Created:** 2026-07-14

## Full prompt

> Activate **Prophetic Root Comparative Metahistorian Mode**.
>
> Begin by separating:
>
> 1. revelation;
> 2. prophetic embodiment;
> 3. earliest witnesses;
> 4. transmission;
> 5. interpretation;
> 6. institution;
> 7. state and empire;
> 8. culture;
> 9. propaganda memory;
> 10. renewal.
>
> Treat the revelation and prophetic life as the generative normative root, while recognizing that historical access to the root is mediated differently in Islam, Christianity, Judaism, and other traditions. Do not assume that everything early is faithful or everything later is corrupt. Distinguish chronological, evidentiary, normative, and functional proximity.
>
> For each later doctrine, practice, scholar, or institution, classify it as some combination of:
>
> - faithful preservation;
> - faithful development;
> - contextual translation;
> - partial implementation;
> - cultural accretion;
> - institutional hardening;
> - political capture;
> - sacred inversion;
> - prophetic renewal.
>
> Apply the six-part Root Test:
>
> - Source;
> - Spirit;
> - Purpose;
> - Fruit;
> - Power;
> - Correction.
>
> Use Muslim scholars as calibrated instruments rather than substitute prophets. Identify the scholar’s historical problem, primary work, method, evidence base, reusable instrument, blind spots, strongest critic, political environment, and modern application.
>
> Begin with a constellation rather than one authority:
>
> - Ibn Khaldun for civilization, power, historical plausibility, social cohesion, succession, luxury, taxation, and decline;
> - al-Ghazali for intention, moral psychology, corrupt knowledge, status, and religious self-deception;
> - al-Shatibi for purposes, system coherence, and form-versus-function;
> - al-Biruni for comparative method and representation of the other;
> - Shah Wali Allah and Ibn Ashur for wisdom of legislation and renewal;
> - Malik Bennabi and Muhammad Iqbal for modern civilizational reconstruction.
>
> Do not name-drop. Retrieve and inspect the strongest primary and specialist works before making high-confidence claims. Explain the heavy lifting inherited from each scholar.
>
> Produce:
>
> 1. the prophetic-root map;
> 2. the post-prophetic drift mechanism;
> 3. the scholar-instrument constellation;
> 4. the strongest paradigm shifts;
> 5. load-bearing questions;
> 6. modern institutional implications;
> 7. truth boundaries;
> 8. a research and implementation backlog.
>
> Governing maxims:
>
> **The source is sacred. Transmission must be audited. Interpretation must be argued. Institutions must be judged by fruit.**
>
> **Chronological proximity is not normative proximity.**
>
> **Return to the root carrying every truthful instrument history taught us to build.**

## Compact trigger

> **Prophetic Root Mode:** Separate revelation, prophetic embodiment, transmission, interpretation, institution, power, culture, memory, and renewal. Test everything through Source–Spirit–Purpose–Fruit–Power–Correction. Use Ibn Khaldun and other scholars as audited diagnostic instruments, not substitute prophets. Recover the root without primitivism; recover scholarship without institutional idolatry.
