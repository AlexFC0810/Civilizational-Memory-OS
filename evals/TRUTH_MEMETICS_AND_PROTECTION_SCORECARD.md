# Truth Memetics and Protection Scorecard

**Status:** Evaluation gate v1  
**Purpose:** Prevent powerful content from becoming propaganda, spectacle, or empty virality.  
**Applies to:** Posters, captions, short videos, carousels, sermons, educational assets, campaign pages, AI-generated visuals, multilingual adaptations, and policy-facing content.

---

## 1. Scoring model

Score each dimension from 0 to 4.

- **0 — Fails:** dangerous, false, exploitative, or absent.
- **1 — Weak:** major gaps; not publishable.
- **2 — Partial:** directionally correct but fragile.
- **3 — Strong:** publishable with minor review.
- **4 — Exemplary:** truth-safe, memorable, dignified, actionable, and institutionally useful.

Maximum score: 64.

### Publication thresholds

- **56–64:** approved after required specialist/localization reviews.
- **48–55:** revise specific weaknesses.
- **36–47:** concept may be salvageable; do not publish.
- **0–35:** reject.

### Automatic failure conditions

Regardless of total score, reject an asset if it:

- contains a materially false claim;
- treats unverified details as fact;
- dehumanizes or collectively blames a population;
- distributes graphic cruelty primarily for engagement;
- uses tragedy as covert conversion pressure;
- implies scientific certainty where evidence is contested;
- exposes or incites harassment of minors;
- contains unverified Qur’anic Arabic, hadith wording, or religious attribution;
- mechanically translates high-risk copy without native review;
- has no safe action pathway when aimed at children;
- makes Islam’s reputation more important than victim protection.

## 2. Dimension A — Truth fidelity

### Questions

- Is the central claim accurate?
- Is the source layer visible?
- Are uncertainty and dispute represented honestly?
- Does the short line survive decompression?
- Are inference and direct evidence distinguished?

### Scoring

**0:** False, fabricated, or grossly misleading.  
**1:** Relies on unsupported certainty or major omission.  
**2:** Mostly accurate but vulnerable to reasonable correction.  
**3:** Accurate, bounded, and source-aware.  
**4:** Accurate under hostile scrutiny with transparent limits and source trail.

---

## 3. Dimension B — Compression integrity

### Questions

- What nuance was removed?
- Did removal reverse or materially alter the truth?
- Can a viewer move from the meme to the full argument?
- Is the claim self-contained enough to resist misreading?

### Scoring

**0:** Slogan depends on distortion.  
**1:** Emotionally effective but conceptually misleading.  
**2:** Carries the gist but loses important boundaries.  
**3:** Strong compression with available expansion.  
**4:** Memorable language that preserves the governing structure exactly.

---

## 4. Dimension C — Moral clarity

### Questions

- Does the asset identify the real moral issue?
- Does it attack a false permission rather than a population?
- Does it distinguish mercy from passivity?
- Does it identify responsibility at the relevant level?

### Scoring

**0:** Confuses or corrupts the moral issue.  
**1:** Generic outrage without analysis.  
**2:** Names harm but not the operating assumption.  
**3:** Exposes one clear false permission.  
**4:** Produces a durable moral reversal the audience can reuse.

---

## 5. Dimension D — Victim dignity

### Questions

- Is the animal or human victim presented as a subject?
- Does the asset avoid replaying humiliation?
- Is the memorial stronger than the perpetrator’s spectacle?
- Is graphic content minimized?

### Scoring

**0:** Exploits or objectifies the victim.  
**1:** Uses suffering primarily as shock material.  
**2:** Respectful intent but still spectacle-dependent.  
**3:** Dignified and non-graphic.  
**4:** Restores relationship, life, and moral presence while converting memory into protection.

---

## 6. Dimension E — Non-dehumanization and symmetry

### Questions

- Does it avoid ethnic, national, religious, or class blame?
- Would we accept the same standard applied to Muslims or allies?
- Does it preserve offender humanity while condemning the act?
- Does it include inward self-audit where appropriate?

### Scoring

**0:** Dehumanizing or collectively accusatory.  
**1:** Thinly disguised population blame.  
**2:** Neutral wording but asymmetric framing.  
**3:** Fair, symmetric, and act-focused.  
**4:** Condemns clearly while actively preventing chauvinism and inviting universal self-audit.

---

## 7. Dimension F — Islamic source integrity

Use only for Islamic assets.

### Questions

- Is Qur’an distinguished from hadith?
- Is hadith authenticity/source status visible?
- Are fiqh, history, and modern practice separated?
- Does the asset avoid implying Muslim behavior defines Islam?
- Is Arabic verified?

### Scoring

**0:** Fabricated or category-collapsed religious claim.  
**1:** Vague religious branding or unreliable attribution.  
**2:** Correct source but weak contextual boundaries.  
**3:** Properly layered and responsibly worded.  
**4:** Source-precise, theologically coherent, self-correcting, and useful for both dawah and Muslim renewal.

---

## 8. Dimension G — Scientific and welfare integrity

### Questions

- Are claims about pain, consciousness, slaughter, stunning, behavior, or trauma supported?
- Are species and method differences respected?
- Does the asset avoid categorical claims beyond evidence?
- Is full-life welfare considered where relevant?

### Scoring

**0:** False or pseudoscientific.  
**1:** Anecdote presented as settled science.  
**2:** Plausible but overly broad.  
**3:** Evidence-aware and appropriately qualified.  
**4:** Integrates best available evidence, uncertainty, and practical welfare implications.

---

## 9. Dimension H — Emotional proportionality

### Questions

- Does emotion serve understanding and action?
- Is fear balanced with mercy, hope, and agency?
- Does the asset avoid rage addiction and helplessness?
- Is grief proportionate and respectful?

### Scoring

**0:** Emotional manipulation or traumatization.  
**1:** Rage bait, guilt pressure, or fear without architecture.  
**2:** Strong emotion but weak regulation.  
**3:** Emotionally resonant and responsible.  
**4:** Produces moral seriousness, hope, courage, and durable motivation without exploitation.

---

## 10. Dimension I — Memetic strength

### Questions

- Is the idea memorable?
- Can it be repeated accurately?
- Does it create a useful identity or question?
- Is it visually compressible?
- Does it distinguish itself from generic advocacy language?

### Scoring

**0:** Confusing or forgettable.  
**1:** Generic slogan.  
**2:** Clear but not distinctive.  
**3:** Memorable and repeatable.  
**4:** High-recall moral language capable of becoming cultural infrastructure.

---

## 11. Dimension J — Identity recoding

### Questions

- What identity does the audience adopt?
- Guardian, trustee, courageous refuser, witness, reformer?
- Does the asset avoid superiority identity?
- Does it make good conduct aspirational?

### Scoring

**0:** Creates hatred, superiority, or voyeurism.  
**1:** Creates only outrage identity.  
**2:** Suggests a positive identity but weakly.  
**3:** Offers a clear guardian identity.  
**4:** Makes protection, restraint, and truth socially desirable and behaviorally specific.

---

## 12. Dimension K — Actionability

### Questions

- Is there a safe concrete next step?
- Is it appropriate to audience and jurisdiction?
- Does it avoid asking children to take dangerous action?
- Does it route evidence or support?

### Scoring

**0:** No action or dangerous action.  
**1:** “Share to raise awareness” only.  
**2:** Generic CTA.  
**3:** Concrete and safe action.  
**4:** Clear action connected to a functioning pathway, institution, or measurable result.

---

## 13. Dimension L — Institutional conversion

### Questions

- Can this asset lead to curriculum, protocol, law, platform change, or welfare improvement?
- Is there an owner for the next step?
- Is the campaign designed beyond content?

### Scoring

**0:** Pure spectacle.  
**1:** Awareness with no conversion path.  
**2:** Institutional aspiration without design.  
**3:** Connected to a real program or protocol.  
**4:** Designed as a front end to durable institutional adoption and outcome measurement.

---

## 14. Dimension M — Localization integrity

### Questions

- Was the asset rewritten rather than mechanically translated?
- Was a native cultural steward involved?
- Are religious, political, class, and emotional meanings understood?
- Does typography and pacing work in-language?

### Scoring

**0:** Dangerous or incoherent translation.  
**1:** Machine translation with no review.  
**2:** Linguistically understandable but culturally foreign.  
**3:** Native-reviewed and culturally legible.  
**4:** Feels born inside the target moral imagination while preserving the canonical truth.

---

## 15. Dimension N — Correction architecture

### Questions

- Is there a source/correction page?
- Is the asset versioned?
- Can experts challenge it?
- Is there a process for public correction?

### Scoring

**0:** No correction possible.  
**1:** Informal willingness only.  
**2:** Contact path but no process.  
**3:** Versioned and correctable.  
**4:** Provenance-aware, transparent, publicly correctable, and linked to the source ledger.

---

## 16. Dimension O — Protection outcome alignment

### Questions

- What protection result is expected?
- Is the asset optimized for more than reach?
- Can the result be measured?
- Does it reduce harm or only narrate it?

### Scoring

**0:** Likely increases harm or spectacle.  
**1:** No outcome theory.  
**2:** Vague benefit.  
**3:** Plausible and measurable protection pathway.  
**4:** Directly connected to verified protection events and feedback loops.

---

## 17. Dimension P — Capture resistance

### Questions

- Could the asset be used for anti-Chinese, anti-atheist, sectarian, nationalist, or commercial propaganda?
- Does it contain safeguards against predictable misuse?
- Can the project apply the principle to itself?

### Scoring

**0:** Designed for capture.  
**1:** Highly vulnerable to misuse.  
**2:** Some guardrails.  
**3:** Clear anti-capture framing.  
**4:** Strong symmetric doctrine, visible self-audit, and correction mechanisms make capture difficult.

---

## 18. Fast pre-publication gate

A fast version for daily assets:

1. **True?**
2. **Fair?**
3. **Dignified?**
4. **Memorable?**
5. **Actionable?**
6. **Safe?**
7. **Native?**
8. **Correctable?**
9. **Self-applicable?**
10. **Protective?**

Any “no” requires revision.

---

## 19. Example scoring — “The voiceless are not ownerless”

### Strengths

- strong compression;
- high recall;
- dignified;
- universal;
- expands into trusteeship and divine ownership;
- adaptable to law and education.

### Risks

- “ownerless” may be interpreted literally as legal ownership;
- some languages cannot preserve the wordplay;
- may require clarification that the doctrine does not erase lawful human care/property categories.

### Required handling

- localize for meaning rather than wordplay;
- pair with explanatory line;
- avoid claiming a settled modern legal-rights theory where the argument is theological and moral.

---

## 20. Example scoring — “Can this honestly be done in the name of the Most Merciful?”

### Strengths

- high Islamic resonance;
- turns ritual into conduct audit;
- strong inward reform;
- reusable across domains.

### Risks

- can be misused as an intuition-only veto against any painful or difficult act;
- may imply the speaker knows divine judgment with certainty;
- requires explanation of necessity, justice, proportionality, and competence.

### Required handling

Pair with:

> The test is not whether the act feels easy. It is whether it is lawful, necessary, proportionate, competent, and free of needless cruelty.

---

## 21. Final evaluation doctrine

> **A powerful meme is not successful because people repeat it. It is successful when the repetition preserves truth, dignifies the vulnerable, changes identity, produces safe action, and becomes accountable protection.**
