## 1. Content mission

> **Create content that makes vulnerable life morally visible, explains the Islamic architecture of mercy and accountability, forms protective behavior, and converts attention into institution.**

Every content unit should perform at least one of five jobs:

1. restore subjecthood;
2. explain a moral principle;
3. form behavior;
4. correct a false narrative;
5. move the audience into concrete protection.

### Stage 4 — Act

- intervene safely;

- support law;
- join the covenant.

### Stage 5 — Build

The audience participates in:

- curriculum;
- institution;
- research;
- policy;

- Muslim renewal.

Do not leave audiences permanently at Stage 1.

## 5. Hook library

### Moral hooks

- What do you do to a creature that cannot report you?
- Does weakness create a claim—or an opportunity?
- The animal cannot testify. Does that mean the crime disappears?
- What would you refuse to do if nobody could ever know?
- The real test of power is what it refuses to do.

### Islamic hooks

- Islam says starving a cat can condemn a soul.
- The Qur’an calls animals communities.
- Why did the Prophet ﷺ order a mother bird’s young returned?
- “Halal” is not the same word as “excellent.”
- Can this honestly be done in the name of the Most Merciful?

### Comparative hooks

- Rules of war look ordinary until you reconstruct the world that needed them.
- Civilization did not end cruelty. It learned to hide authorship.
- Nomadic solidarity can be powerful—and still exclude outsiders.
- The Mongols had extraordinary cohesion. Cohesion is not morality.
- Technology increases power, not wisdom.

### Child hooks

- You may be the only one who says stop.
- Filming cruelty is not staying neutral.
- Strong people protect what cannot fight back.
- Be the one who protects.

## 8. The advocate method

Advocacy is not neutrality between truth and falsehood.

But truth-loyal advocacy refuses brittle claims.

Process:

1. identify the strongest criticism;
2. separate source from violator;
3. concede genuine failure;
4. reconstruct historical baseline;
5. show the Islamic architecture;
6. compare symmetrically;
7. state uncertainty;
8. return to the governing principle.

Example:

Bad defense:

> Every halal slaughter is painless.

Truth-loyal defense:

> Islam makes minimization of suffering a religious duty even where slaughter is lawful. Muslim practice should be continuously improved using the best evidence about consciousness, handling, and technique.

## 9. Anti-outrage design

Every atrocity-related post must include at least one of:

- reporting instruction;
- child action;
- law proposal;
- welfare standard;
- partner organization;
- curriculum link;
- verified case update;
- non-graphic memorial.

Never publish only:

- gore;
- rage;
- helplessness;
- national blame;
- offender dehumanization;
- engagement bait.

> **Outrage is ignition fuel, not infrastructure.**

## 14. Editorial quality gates

Before publication:

- Is the claim sourced?
- Is the source layer visible?
- Is the victim dignified?
- Is the language culturally native?
- Does the post create action?
- Does it apply the standard inward?
- Is any uncertainty hidden?
- Could it incite ethnic blame?
- Could it reward cruelty content?
- Does it make Islam clearer rather than merely more marketable?
