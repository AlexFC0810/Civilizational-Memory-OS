## 14. CTA bank

### Individual

- Report rather than repost.
- Teach the refusal steps.
- Audit one practice under your control.
- Ask one full-life welfare question.
- Sign the Voiceless Covenant.
- Support a credible local rescuer.
- Study the source packet.

### Parent

- Tell one animal-mercy story this week.
- Rehearse what your child should do if peers hurt an animal.
- Explain why filming cruelty is not neutral.

### School

- Adopt Be the One Who Protects.
- Create a confidential reporting path.
- Train staff on child animal-cruelty response.

### Faith community

- Teach animal mercy from primary sources.
- Audit food, events, and local animal practices.
- Pair outward teaching with internal reform.

### Platform

- Remove public spectacle.
- Preserve secure evidence.
- Detect reuploads and monetization.
- Publish transparency data.

### Government

- Define cruelty around inflicted suffering, not only property damage.
- Create intervention pathways for minors.
- Track enforcement and recurrence.

## 15. Prohibited lines

- “Islam invented animal rights.”
- “All halal slaughter is painless.”
- “Chinese people do not care about animals.”
- “Atheists have no reason to be moral.”
- “Children who hurt animals will become serial killers.”
- “This one case proves a civilization is barbaric.”
- “Watching the full video is necessary to understand.”
- “If you do not share this, you do not care.”
- “God will torture you” as an isolated child-facing message.
- “Animals and humans are exactly the same.”
- “Every traditional practice is cruelty.”
- “Every modern welfare system is humane.”
