# Anti-Idolatry Proof Stack

**Status:** Research source ledger  
**Use case:** Ground the anti-idolatry framework in psychology, scripture, history, and modern institutional behavior.  
**Core rule:** Every grand claim about anti-idolatry should be backed by proof from at least one of four layers: psychology, scripture, history, or modern case studies.

---

## 1. Purpose

The anti-idolatry thesis is powerful but can sound abstract unless grounded.

Core thesis:

> Anti-idolatry is not merely theology. It is civilizational protection against tyranny, genocide, exploitation, ego-worship, collective guilt, and the capture of sacred language by power.

This ledger tracks the proof stack needed to solidify that thesis.

---

## 2. Four proof layers

### Layer A — Psychology

How humans obey, conform, dehumanize, worship status, submit to authority, and rationalize cruelty.

### Layer B — Scripture / moral architecture

How sacred texts dethrone false absolutes, constrain rulers, protect the vulnerable, reject collective guilt, and extend moral concern.

### Layer C — Historical examples

How divine kingship, imperial cult, nationalism, racism, state worship, and ideology become destructive when treated as ultimate.

### Layer D — Modern institutional behavior

How states, parties, markets, media systems, bureaucracies, and identity movements weaponize sacred narratives or substitute secular absolutes.

## 8. Public-facing guardrails

When using this thesis publicly:

- Critique idolatry, not entire peoples.
- Critique actions, institutions, policies, and ideologies, not ethnic or religious essence.
- Avoid phrases with histories of collective demonization.
- Do not use sacred critique to create a new idolatry.
- Always preserve the individual moral-accountability guardrail.
- Do not map ancient enemy categories onto modern civilian populations.
- Distinguish Judaism from Zionism, Islam from Muslim states, Christianity from Christendom, secularism from secular empires.

## 9. Strong public formulation

> Anti-idolatry is the refusal to let any created thing become ultimate. When nation, race, state, land, market, tribe, appetite, ideology, or religious institution becomes ultimate, people become sacrificial material. The Abrahamic guardrail says every power must answer to God, justice, mercy, and the moral worth of each soul.
