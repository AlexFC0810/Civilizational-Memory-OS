# People of the Qiblah — Rapprochement and Momentum Source Ledger

**As of:** 2026-07-14  
**Status:** Active source ledger; current-institution claims must be rechecked before publication  
**Purpose:** Distinguish established precedents, living momentum, announced implementation, and unverified outcomes.

## Working Verdict

The bridge already exists in fragments. The strongest precedents establish a legal-theological floor against takfir, demonstrate that sustained Sunni-Shia scholarly collaboration is possible, and now explicitly call for a “People of the Qiblah” framework that preserves doctrinal pluralism while institutionalizing cooperation.

The missing layer is not another declaration. It is a non-state-owned, digitally native, multilingual implementation system combining provenance-aware history, education, AI auditing, media immunity, shared work, governance, and durable public infrastructure.

## Confidence Scale

- **Established:** directly supported by an authoritative primary or institutional source.
- **Strong:** supported by authoritative institutional and/or high-quality academic sources.
- **Provisional:** plausible and source-supported but incomplete or time-sensitive.
- **Unverified:** announced, claimed, or inferred but not confirmed through a reliable outcome record.

## 1. The Amman Message

### Claim

The Amman Message created a major modern recognition floor across Sunni, Shia, and Ibadi schools, forbade intra-Muslim takfir among recognized adherents, and articulated qualifications for issuing fatwas.

### Status

**Established.**

### Evidence

The official Amman Message site states that the initiative began in November 2004, that 200 scholars from 50 countries met in July 2005, and that they recognized eight legal schools of Sunni, Shia, and Ibadi Islam, prohibited takfir among Muslims covered by that definition, and defined preconditions for issuing fatwas. It reports more than 500 scholarly endorsements and subsequent adoption by multiple international assemblies.

### Source

- Official site: https://ammanmessage.com/

### Strategic significance

The Amman Message provides something close to a constitutional minimum for intra-Islamic recognition.

### Limitation

Its own official summary acknowledges that the achievement remains principial unless implemented through treaties, law, curricula, imam training, publishing, and multimedia. This is the central implementation gap.

### Repository interpretation

> **The Amman Message supplied a constitutional floor, but not a complete civilizational operating system.**

## 2. Twentieth-Century *Taqrib* and Dar al-Taqrib

### Claim

The twentieth-century *taqrib* movement demonstrated that sustained Sunni-Shia scholarly dialogue, joint publication, and engagement with classical and contemporary sources were possible.

### Status

**Strong.**

### Evidence

Harvard's Project on Shi'ism and Global Affairs describes *taqrib* as the most recent large-scale iteration of Shia-Sunni ecumenical relations and peacebuilding in the Middle East and states that it produced sustained scholarly dialogue, joint publications, and flourishing engagement with Islamic sources.

### Source

- Harvard Divinity School / Weatherhead Center report page: https://shiism.hds.harvard.edu/publications/legacies-islamic-ecumenicismtaqrib-shia-sunni-relations-and

### Strategic significance

*Taqrib* proves that elite scholarly rapprochement can become more than ceremonial dialogue.

### Limitation

Further source work is required on:

- dependence on political conditions and state relationships;
- internal disagreement over doctrinal recognition;
- institutional continuity after key personalities died;
- the exact relationship between Dar al-Taqrib, Al-Azhar, Qom, and later state-backed proximity organizations;
- and why the movement did not become a distributed mass institution.

### Repository interpretation

> **Dialogue without distributed institutions, independent funding, and public implementation remains fragile.**

## 3. The 2025 “Call for the People of Qiblah”

### Claim

A major 2025 intra-Islamic conference explicitly adopted a People-of-the-Qiblah frame that rejects forced doctrinal uniformity, affirms legitimate diversity, prohibits sectarian insult and harm, and calls for youth, digital, educational, scholarly, media, and institutional implementation.

### Status

**Established.**

### Evidence

The official declaration states that the conference was held in Bahrain on February 19–20, 2025, following consultations with Sunni and Shia authorities and participation by hundreds of scholars, religious authorities, and intellectuals. It explicitly says that dissolving schools into one or imposing uniformity is neither possible nor a goal. It affirms doctrinal pluralism, dialogue, mutual understanding, respect for sacred figures, prohibition of harm, and study of recognized scholars across schools.

### Primary source

- Official English declaration PDF: https://www.muslim-elders.com/Files/EventFile/D8WXB00MVY_Call_for_the_People_of_Qiblah_EN.pdf

### Key source-supported principles

- unity does not require erasing doctrinal difference;
- theological and jurisprudential diversity is a legitimate historical reality;
- mutual understanding should operate at scholarly and media levels;
- intellectual freedom and qualified ijtihad should be protected;
- sectarian loyalty must not become fanaticism that overrides the Ummah;
- insults against revered figures of any school should end;
- harm based on sect, ethnicity, nationality, historical position, or scholarly interpretation is prohibited;
- intra-Islamic understanding should precede or accompany wider interfaith dialogue;
- curricula should study recognized scholars and contributions across schools.

### Strategic significance

This is the clearest high-level institutional convergence with the repository's emerging doctrine:

> **Protected difference below; shared civilization above.**

### Important originality note

The repository does not claim to have invented the phrase “People of the Qiblah.” The distinctive contribution is the synthesis of that inherited and currently active frame into a non-state-owned civilizational commons, historical intelligence architecture, AI constitution, propaganda immune system, shared-work layer, and anti-capture governance model.

## 4. The 2025 Conference Recommendations

### Claim

The official recommendations move beyond abstract unity and call for systematic documentation of points of agreement, curricula, textbooks on the jurisprudence of diversity, anti-hate platforms, joint civilizational initiatives, youth-oriented digital strategy, specialized committees, a League for Intra-Islamic Dialogue, and an implementation committee.

### Status

**Established as recommendations; implementation remains provisional.**

### Evidence

The conference recommendations call for:

- constructive dialogue rather than theological reconciliation;
- cooperation among religious, scholarly, intellectual, and media institutions;
- a comprehensive project documenting shared beliefs, jurisprudence, and values;
- institutionalization through curricula, sermons, media, and policy;
- textbooks teaching the jurisprudence of diversity;
- platforms countering hate speech;
- joint social, developmental, and civilizational initiatives;
- youth and digital strategies;
- inter-school youth programs, including Muslims in the West;
- a unified discourse inspired by the People-of-the-Qiblah charter;
- a League for Intra-Islamic Dialogue;
- and a joint committee to implement the resolutions.

### Source

- Official conference recommendations: https://gciid.com/conference-recommendations/

### Strategic significance

The recommendations identify many of the correct implementation domains. The repository's opportunity is to build the missing technical and institutional substrate rather than duplicate the conference.

### Limitation

Recommendation does not equal execution. Every claimed committee, league, curriculum, or second conference must be independently verified before being described as operational.

## 6. Other Living Organizations and Efforts

### Current state

Multiple organizations, clerics, universities, state-backed forums, and civil-society actors use the language of Islamic unity, proximity, anti-takfir, or sectarian de-escalation.

### Status

**Research frontier.**

### Required audit dimensions

For each organization, determine:

- governance and school representation;
- state affiliation;
- donor and security-service exposure;
- whether it produces scholarship, curricula, public tools, or only conferences;
- evidence of youth participation;
- evidence of cross-school public-benefit work;
- ability to criticize its own sponsor or tradition;
- multilingual reach;
- continuity beyond founding personalities;
- and measurable outcomes.

Do not infer legitimacy from branding alone.

## Structural Synthesis

1. **A legal-theological floor against takfir.**
2. **Recognition that unity does not require doctrinal merger.**
3. **The People-of-the-Qiblah frame itself.**
4. **Calls for youth, digital platforms, curricula, and media reform.**
5. **Recognition that shared civilizational work is necessary.**
6. **A growing need for school-aware AI and provenance systems.**

### What remains missing

1. A trusted public source graph for disputed Islamic history.
2. A multilingual, school-aware Islamic AI constitution and benchmark.
3. Distributed governance independent of one state or sect.
4. Transparent funding and anti-capture rules.
5. Public implementation metrics.
6. A shared-work portfolio that makes fraternity empirically visible.
7. A durable digital-waqf architecture that survives leadership and platform changes.
