# Source Ledger — Byzantium, Golden Ages, and Prophetic Civilization

**Status:** Research seed; not source-closed  
**Created:** 2026-07-13  
**Purpose:** Atomize the factual, causal, and interpretive claims produced by the Byzantium / Golden Ages conversation so no provisional synthesis is mistaken for settled history.

## Status vocabulary

- **Established:** Strongly supported and unlikely to change materially.
- **Probable:** Better supported than alternatives but causal weight or scope remains uncertain.
- **Contested:** Serious scholarly disagreement or definitional dependence.
- **Unresolved:** Insufficient evidence reviewed.
- **Unsupported:** No adequate evidence currently identified.
- **Category error:** The claim combines unlike things or uses a misleading concept.

## J. Prohibited public claims pending closure

Do not publish as settled:

- “Byzantium was the most successful empire in history.”
- “Byzantium flourished because Orthodoxy is true.”
- “Byzantium had a gold standard.”
- “Christianity invented hospitals.”
- “The earliest Christians only called themselves followers of the Way.”
- “Judaism is outside the Abrahamic bridge.”
- “The Islamic Golden Age ended in 1258.”
- “Golden-age achievement proves moral superiority.”
- “Every Byzantine charitable institution was caused by Christianity.”
- “Every Byzantine atrocity was merely Roman corruption and says nothing about Christian institutions or texts.”

## K. Source hierarchy for closure

### Tier 1 — Primary and near-primary

- imperial law codes and novellae;
- council canons and ecclesial legislation;
- liturgical and theological texts;
- hospital, monastery, orphanage, and endowment charters;
- tax, property, court, and manumission records;
- coins and numismatic evidence;
- inscriptions and archaeology;
- contemporary chronicles from multiple communities;
- Acts and earliest Christian texts in critical editions;
- Jewish sources from Second Temple and rabbinic periods;
- Qur'anic and early Islamic sources for path and civilizational norms.

### Tier 2 — Specialist scholarship

- university-press monographs;
- peer-reviewed journals;
- specialist encyclopedias;
- archaeological reports;
- histories of medicine, slavery, law, philanthropy, and institutions;
- scholarship representing competing interpretations.

### Tier 3 — Synthesis and public scholarship

- reputable museum essays;
- high-quality university educational resources;
- reference works;
- carefully sourced public histories.

### Non-terminal evidence

- interviews;
- apologetic videos;
- social posts;
- unsourced quote graphics;
- Wikipedia alone;
- AI synthesis alone.
